# Research

## Corpus findings

- The workspace contains a legacy guide naming 15 live GPT concepts, a nine-companion production spec, a completed pipeline assistant Action design, two GPT packs, a detailed Realtor co-marketing system, mortgage education and compliance knowledge, persona/voice systems, a podcast prompt, and content-production workflows.
- The strongest source depth is in Realtor co-marketing, pipeline operations, mortgage content production, brand/voice, and compliance QA.
- Exact copies exist across exported GPT packs and four Realtor knowledge directories. Version drift is more important than missing ideas.
- Several legacy public concepts make risky promises or invite personalized credit/qualification advice. They require consolidation and tighter public boundaries.

## Portfolio implications

- The first builds should reuse mature sources and avoid live consumer rate or eligibility decisions.
- Internal pipeline/processing and partner co-marketing have the clearest near-term business return.
- Public mortgage assistants need source freshness, licensed handoff, jurisdiction controls, and compliance approval before launch.
- Shared knowledge governance is a prerequisite, not cleanup work after launch.

See the three reports in `docs/reports/` for the complete source catalog, consolidation decisions, and ranked recommendations.
