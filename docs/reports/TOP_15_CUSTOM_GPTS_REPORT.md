# Top 15 Legends Custom GPTs — decision report

**Decision owner:** GOAT Architect

**Prepared:** 2026-07-17

**Portfolio boundary:** recommendations only; no individual GPT was built in this foundation mission.

## Executive recommendation

Build internal and partner-facing leverage before public mortgage advice. The first five should be: **Legends Pipeline & Processing Copilot**, **Legends Realtor Co-Marketing & Partner Growth Studio**, **Legends Mortgage Content Repurposing Studio**, **Legends Team Growth & Coaching Assistant**, and **Legends Webinar Campaign Builder**.

This order starts with a mature internal workflow, then expands partner revenue and content production using the strongest existing source material. The public Borrower Education & Lead Concierge ranks lower despite high potential because it needs stricter freshness, licensing, privacy, and compliance controls.

## Scoring model

Each factor is scored 1–10 and weighted equally. The maximum opportunity score is 150. A higher number is always favorable.

| Code | Factor | Meaning of 10 |
|---|---|---|
| BI | Business impact | Transformational measurable impact |
| FU | Frequency of use | Daily / near-daily |
| TS | Time saved | Large recurring time reduction |
| RP | Revenue potential | Strong direct or influenced revenue |
| RV | Realtor relationship value | Material partner growth/retention |
| MT | Mortgage team value | Broad, meaningful team leverage |
| RU | Reusability | Reusable across roles/users |
| SQ | Source quality | Mature, relevant existing sources |
| BE | Build ease | Easy to build; inverse of difficulty |
| LI | Live-data independence | Little need for live data/Actions; inverse of dependence |
| CS | Compliance safety | Low consumer/compliance risk; inverse of risk |
| DF | Differentiation | Clearly better than generic ChatGPT |
| MA | Maintainability | Easy ownership and updating |
| LS | Launch speed | Fast path to a usable pilot |
| BF | Legends brand fit | Excellent strategic and voice fit |

Ties are resolved by near-term workflow evidence, internal/partner safety, and strategic sequencing.

## Reusable Custom GPT package template

Use the structure in `docs/goat_architect/Architecture.md` and the checklist in `gpts/templates/README.md`. The package must make audience, owner, business outcome, capability settings, data boundary, knowledge versions, Action permissions, compliance gates, tests, examples, and rollback explicit. Do not ship prompt-only GPTs.

## Repository naming conventions

- GPT directory: lowercase kebab case, e.g. `gpts/legends-pipeline-processing-copilot/`.
- Display name: `Legends <clear job>`, avoiding vague “guru,” “master,” or “expert” unless the brand owner deliberately approves it.
- Package files: fixed lowercase names from the architecture; knowledge snapshots use `domain-topic-vYYYY-MM-DD.md`.
- Action operation IDs: `legends.<domain>.<verb>.v1`.
- Tests: `test-<risk-or-workflow>-NN.md`; synthetic fixtures only.
- Tags: `<gpt-slug>-vMAJOR.MINOR.PATCH`.
- Archive: preserve the old version and link to the replacement; never silently overwrite provenance.

## Versioning system

Use semantic versioning for each GPT package:

- **MAJOR** — audience, scope, risk boundary, instruction contract, or Action behavior changes.
- **MINOR** — backward-compatible capability, workflow, knowledge, or test additions.
- **PATCH** — wording, example, metadata, and non-behavioral corrections.

Version volatile knowledge separately with an effective date and content hash. An approved GPT release records the exact instruction version, knowledge snapshot versions, Action schema versions, model/capability assumptions, and test result. Emergency compliance changes create at least a patch and trigger targeted regression tests.

## Risks of public-facing mortgage GPTs

- Stale rates, guidelines, program availability, licensing, or disclosures.
- Personalized statements that users interpret as approval, official quote, or financial/credit/legal/tax advice.
- Fair Housing or steering problems from protected-class or neighborhood language.
- TILA/Reg Z trigger terms without required context; RESPA issues in partner content; CAN-SPAM/TCPA consent/opt-out failures.
- Unnecessary collection or leakage of borrower PII; prompt injection or cross-user data exposure through Actions.
- Hallucinated property, licensing, guarantee, or market facts.
- Inadequate disclosure that the assistant is AI and not an underwriter or official decision maker.
- Inaccessible or English-only experiences that create inconsistent treatment.
- Autonomous sends/publishing before a licensed human and compliance process review the content.
