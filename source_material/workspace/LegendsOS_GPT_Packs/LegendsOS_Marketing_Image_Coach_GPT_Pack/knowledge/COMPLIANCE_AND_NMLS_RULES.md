# Compliance and NMLS Rules

The detailed compliance reference. The plain-English version is in `MORTGAGE_MARKETING_SAFETY_RULES.md`. Both files agree — this one goes deeper.

## NMLS disclosure

Every piece of mortgage marketing should carry the NMLS line.

**Default Legends disclosure line:**
*Jeremy McDonald, NMLS 1195266, The Legends Mortgage Team powered by Loan Factory, NMLS 320841.*

If an individual LO is on the post, use their NMLS instead of Jeremy's, but Loan Factory's company NMLS (320841) stays.

**Placement:** small, legible, bottom of the image. Doesn't have to dominate, but must be readable on the device the post is viewed on.

## APR equal-prominence rule

If a marketing piece quotes a rate (e.g., "6.25%"), the APR must appear with **equal prominence** — same font size, same visual weight, same level of visibility. Burying APR in fine print is a violation.

**Example, correct:**
> 6.25% rate. 6.45% APR.

**Example, wrong:**
> **6.25%** rate. (Then in 4-pt grey: *6.45% APR*.)

If the image is general lifestyle marketing without rate numbers, the APR rule doesn't apply.

## Banned or restricted claims

These need substantiation or rewrite:

| Claim | Rule |
|---|---|
| "Lowest rate" | Requires apples-to-apples substantiation. Rewrite to "competitive" unless documented. |
| "Best loan" / "Best mortgage" | Subjective superlative. Rewrite. |
| "Guaranteed approval" | No lender guarantees approval. Always rewrite. |
| "No closing costs" | Costs exist; they may be lender-paid. Rewrite to "lender-paid closing cost options available on qualifying programs." |
| "Free" | Same as "no closing costs." Rewrite or qualify. |
| "Save $X/month" | A specific dollar claim requires a specific borrower's file. Don't put in general marketing. |
| "Pre-approval in 24 hours" | Often true but timing depends on file completeness. Soften to "fast pre-approvals on complete files." |
| "We work with anyone" / "Any credit score" | False. Rewrite to "we work with a wide range of credit profiles." |

## Substantiated specifics (when the LO has them)

If the LO can show source data — a specific rate sheet, a documented offer, a verified comparison — they can use specific numbers in marketing. The image must still:

- Show APR equally prominent.
- Include the NMLS line.
- Avoid "lowest" / "best" superlatives.
- Avoid borrower-specific savings claims.

## Fair lending considerations

- No images suggesting one demographic group is favored or disfavored.
- Use diverse imagery in family / buyer / borrower scenes when generating people.
- Never imply that approval depends on race, ethnicity, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, or familial status.

## RESPA / referral hazards

- Don't imply that a Realtor (or any settlement service provider) is preferred in exchange for referrals.
- Co-branded posts with Realtors are fine — they need to look like partnership marketing, not a referral incentive.
- Don't show "we get you to the closing table together" language tied to a specific Realtor's volume or revenue.

## Truth-in-Lending / Reg Z signal

If the image mentions any of these, the APR rule and disclosure rules kick in:

- A specific rate.
- A specific payment amount.
- A specific down payment.
- A specific term (e.g., "30-year").
- "Refinance and save."
- "Cash out."

When in doubt, leave the numbers out of the image and let the caption (with full compliance language) carry them.

## Honest comparisons

If marketing compares Legends/Loan Factory to "the big banks" or "online lenders" in any way, the comparison must be specific, accurate, and the LO must be able to back it up. Otherwise: rewrite.

## Substitution table — quick reference

| If LO writes | Substitute with |
|---|---|
| "Lowest rate around" | "Let's see what fits." |
| "Guaranteed approval" | "Let's review your options." |
| "No closing costs" | "Lender-paid closing cost programs available." |
| "Save $X/month" | "Curious what a refi could look like? Let's run the math." |
| "Best mortgage company" | "Local team. Real service." |

## When this GPT spots a violation

Rewrite the visual concept on the spot. Don't ask the LO permission first — just fix it and tell them what changed and why.
