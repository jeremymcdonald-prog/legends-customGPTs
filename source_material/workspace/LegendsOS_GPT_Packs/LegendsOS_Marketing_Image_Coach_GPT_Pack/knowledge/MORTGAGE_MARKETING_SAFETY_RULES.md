# Mortgage Marketing Safety Rules

Plain-English version of the rules. Use these every time. The full
compliance reference is in `COMPLIANCE_AND_NMLS_RULES.md`.

## The top six rules

1. **NMLS goes on mortgage marketing.** Default line:
   *Jeremy McDonald, NMLS 1195266, The Legends Mortgage Team powered by Loan Factory, NMLS 320841.*
2. **If you say a rate, you also say the APR.** Equal prominence — same size, same visibility.
3. **Don't say "lowest" or "best."** Unless the LO has substantiation (an apples-to-apples comparison with a citation), rewrite the claim.
4. **Don't say "guaranteed approval."** No lender guarantees approval. Use "let's see what fits" / "let's review your options" language.
5. **Don't say "no closing costs" or "free."** Closing costs always exist somewhere; they may be lender-paid or borrower-paid. If you mean lender-paid, say "lender-paid closing costs available on qualifying programs."
6. **Don't promise a specific payment savings without source data.** "Save $300/month" is not a marketing claim — it's a quote, and a quote needs the specific borrower's file behind it.

## Things you CAN say comfortably

- "Let's see what programs fit your situation."
- "I help first-time buyers, refinance shoppers, and investors."
- "I'll walk you through every option."
- "Pre-approvals are how we start."
- "Programs available for self-employed, VA, FHA, conventional, jumbo, and DSCR." (Adjust for what the team actually does.)
- General lifestyle imagery without numbers — front porches, key handoffs, families in homes, neighborhood streets — is always safe.

## Things you should rewrite if the LO asks

| LO asks for | Safer rewrite |
|---|---|
| "Lowest rates in town" | "Competitive options — let's run your scenario." |
| "Guaranteed approval" | "Let's see what fits." |
| "Save $500/month, no questions asked" | "Curious what a refi could look like? Let's run the math." |
| "No closing costs, no fees" | "Lender-paid closing cost options on qualifying programs." |
| "Get pre-approved in 24 hours" | "Most pre-approvals turn around fast — depending on your file." |

## When the LO has substantiation

If the LO can show the source ("our rate today is X.XX% APR Y.YY% on a 30-year conventional for a 740 score borrower"), they can use specific numbers — but the image must show APR equally prominently and include the NMLS line.

## Negative-prompt staples

Every mortgage image prompt should include these in the negative prompt to avoid common AI mistakes:

```
no fake brand logos, no fake company names, no readable financial numbers,
no fake APR or rate text, no extra fingers, no warped faces, no
copyright-protected brand assets, no celebrity faces, no real fake addresses
```

## When in doubt

Default to lifestyle imagery + Legends NMLS line. You can't be wrong with "family on a front porch + key handoff + warm sunlight + NMLS line in the corner."
