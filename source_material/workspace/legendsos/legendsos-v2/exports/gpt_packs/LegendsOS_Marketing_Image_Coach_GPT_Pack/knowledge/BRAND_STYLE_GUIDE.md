# Brand Style Guide — Legends Visual Identity

Internal style reference. Use these when "Legends brand" is the requested vibe.

## Core aesthetic

**Premium dark-gold-glass command-center.** Think: the lobby of a refined investment firm, lit at golden hour, with a single warm spotlight on a piece of polished hardware. Black charcoal background, champagne gold accents, refined glass texture, subtle dark blue trust cues, off-white type.

If a prompt comes out looking "fintech-bright" or "consumer-app-poppy," it's wrong. Restart with darker, warmer, more refined direction.

## Color palette

| Color | Use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Black charcoal `#0E0E10` to `#1A1A1F` | Backgrounds | Default background everywhere. Never pure black `#000000` — that reads cheap. |
| Champagne gold `#C9A85F` to `#E5C880` | Accents, key visual highlights, gold lines | Warm metallic, not bright yellow. Think gold leaf, not highlighter. |
| Off-white `#F3EFE6` to `#F8F4EB` | Type, key callouts | Slightly warm white. Avoids harsh pure white. |
| Trust blue `#1F2C3C` to `#28384A` | Subtle highlight, secondary accent | Used sparingly — mostly to balance gold with cooler depth in trust-anchored pieces. |
| Dim gray `#3A3A40` | Dividers, secondary lines | Used for fine geometric elements. |

Avoid: neon green, hot pink, candy red, electric purple, baby blue, beige, beige beige.

## Typography vibe

(This guide doesn't ship a typeface to the AI — image models can't reliably render specific fonts. Describe the *vibe*.)

- **Headlines:** "modern sans-serif, slight serif accent on key letters, refined editorial weight."
- **Body / subhead:** "clean sans-serif, comfortable line-height, never cramped."
- **Numbers:** "tabular numerals when comparing values, otherwise normal sans."

If the AI puts garbled text in the image, ask for a text-light composition and add the real text afterwards in Image Studio or Canva (only if Canva is approved for that LO).

## Composition rules

- **One focal subject.** Don't crowd the frame.
- **Asymmetric balance.** Subject left-of-center, headline space right. Avoid dead-center compositions for feed images (feels like a stock photo).
- **Plenty of negative space.** Leave room for a headline overlay.
- **Subject on a level visual plane.** Avoid weird Dutch angles unless the brief specifically asks for energy.
- **Light from the upper-left or directly front.** Avoid hard side lighting that puts half the face in shadow.

## Texture cues

- "Refined glass" — subtle reflections, soft edge highlights.
- "Warm gold leaf" — slight grain in the gold accents, never flat.
- "Cinematic grading" — slightly desaturated, mood-heavy, but never crushed.

## Bad-vibe red flags

If the output reads any of these, the brand prompt was wrong:

- Bright corporate stock photo with everyone smiling at the camera.
- Y2K neon glow.
- Crypto-fintech "to the moon" vibe.
- Pastel candy colors.
- Heavy gradient backgrounds (purple-to-blue, etc.).
- Cartoon-flat illustration with no depth.

## Good-vibe checklist

- Dark, warm, intentional.
- One subject, well-lit.
- Gold feels metallic, not screen-yellow.
- Visual hierarchy is obvious (headline first, subject second, support third).
- Looks like it could land on the wall of a private office, not a billboard.

## Variations

If the brief asks for "off-brand on purpose" (e.g., a playful Realtor team-up that needs to feel warmer), allow the LO to dial up the **Lifestyle Warm** preset. Don't force dark-gold-glass when warm photographic is the right call.

## Two-line summary

Dark charcoal + champagne gold. One subject. Quiet luxury, not crypto-fintech.
