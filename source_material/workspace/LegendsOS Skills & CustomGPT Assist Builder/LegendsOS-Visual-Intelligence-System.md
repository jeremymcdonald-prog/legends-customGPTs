# 2\. Visual Brand DNA

**Backgrounds:** Dark charcoal and near-black. Cinematic depth. Blurred premium interiors, moody neighborhoods at dusk, command-center offices. Never plain white.

**Accents:** Burnt / Loan Factory orange for energy and CTAs. Champagne gold for authority and signature moments. Used as accents, not fills. One accent leads per image.

**Typography color:** White and silver for headlines. Gold for premium/script moments. Orange reserved for the single most important word or the CTA.

**Feel:** Premium mortgage command center. Cinematic realism, not cartoon, not stock-flyer. Clean directional lighting on the face. Strong face presence (Jeremy or the LO is a real presence, not a tiny circle footer). Luxury but not flashy. Bold but compliant.

**Color system (lock these):**

- Charcoal `#1A1A1A` / Black `#0A0A0A`  
- Loan Factory Orange `#F26A21` (approximate; match the official brand orange)  
- Champagne Gold `#C9A24B`  
- White `#FFFFFF` / Silver `#C7C7C7`

**Texture:** Subtle film grain, soft bokeh, controlled lens flare on orange. No heavy gradients, no rainbow, no clip-art icons.

**What this DNA replaces:** white backgrounds, circular headshot footers, crowded copy blocks, cartoon mascots, chrome word-art logos, named-competitor gags.

# 3\. Image Category System (Three Engines)

### Engine A — Cinematic Authority

**Purpose:** Build trust, authority, leadership, premium recognition. **Look:** Dark luxury, Jeremy/LO confident and centered, gold \+ charcoal, minimal text, clean compliance strip. **Use for:** market updates, AI authority, team wins, veteran closing day, Friday momentum, business growth, Realtor partnership, leadership posts. **Source proof:** Friday Momentum, Rate Update, AI Command Center, Veteran Closing Day.

### Engine B — Pattern Interrupt

**Purpose:** Stop the scroll with emotion, contrast, tension, or surprise. **Look:** High contrast, a single charged emotion or visual conflict, orange energy. Professional, never cheesy, never disparaging a named competitor. **Use for:** bank vs broker, credit myths, debt traps, rate confusion, buyer mistakes, Realtor pain points, AI disruption, mortgage myths. **Use sparingly.** Rule: never name or depict a real competitor (no Wells Fargo, no Chase, no zombie banks). Use the category ("the big bank," "the retail lender") not the brand. The bank-zombie image is the cautionary example: garbled text, named competitors, zero branding. Do not repeat it.

### Engine C — Educational Minimal

**Purpose:** Teach one mortgage idea fast. **Look:** One color-blocked text zone, one big idea, lots of breathing room, mobile-first. Jeremy/LO present but secondary to the lesson. **Use for:** FHA, VA, USDA, DPA, credit, rates, investor loans, refinance, DSCR, bank statement loans, reverse mortgage, buyer readiness. **Source proof:** Rate Update, Money Moves Monday.

# 5\. Typography System

One headline, one support line. That is the default. If you need a third line, you are making a flyer, stop.

| Level | Role | Style | Color |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Primary Headline | The one big idea | Heavy condensed sans (e.g. Anton / Archivo Black), uppercase or strong title case | White, with orange on the single key word |
| Secondary Hook | One supporting line | Medium sans (e.g. Montserrat SemiBold) | Silver / white |
| Premium Accent | Brand moments, "Momentum," "Closing Day" | Elegant script or high-contrast serif | Champagne gold |
| Proof Line | Stat, credential, or sub-benefit | Small caps sans | Orange or silver |
| CTA | The action | Bold sans in an orange chip or underline | White on orange, or orange on dark |
| Compliance Footer | NMLS, EHO, disclaimer | Clean small sans, readable, never decorative | Silver / light gray on dark |
| Logo Lockup | Legends \+ Powered by Loan Factory | Official lockup only | As provided |

Rules: max two type families per image plus the official logo. Never stretch or recolor the Loan Factory logo. Compliance text must be legible at phone size, never below the readable threshold. Do not overload text.

```
[AUDIENCE] — who this speaks to (first-time buyer, veteran, investor, Realtor partner, refi homeowner)

[MORTGAGE TOPIC] — the single idea (VA loan, credit, rate move, DPA, AI workflow)

[ASPECT RATIO] — 1:1, 9:16, 16:9, or banner per platform
[NEGATIVE PROMPT] — see default below
[OUTPUT PURPOSE] — platform + post type
```

# 11\. AI Boardroom Review Workflow (Visual Board)

Every concept is reviewed by eight internal lenses before it publishes. These are internal strategic lenses, not impersonations.

| Lens | Checks |
| :---- | :---- |
| Marketing Strategist | Does it stop the scroll and serve a goal? |
| Compliance Reviewer | Does it pass every rule in Section 7? |
| Mortgage Expert | Is the mortgage idea accurate and current? |
| Consumer Psychology | Does the emotion land for the real buyer? |
| Realtor Relationship | Is it RESPA-safe and partner-friendly? |

| Social Performance | Will it read at thumbnail and drive action? |
| Conversion Strategist | Is there one clear CTA and next step? |

### Default Review Output (PASTE)

```
1. EXECUTIVE VERDICT — publish / fix-then-publish / hold
2. ATTENTION SCORE (1-10) — scroll-stopping power
3. COMPLIANCE SCORE (1-10) — 10 means fully clear; under 8 cannot publish
4. TRUST SCORE (1-10) — credibility and proof
5. READABILITY SCORE (1-10) — clarity at phone size
6. BRAND SCORE (1-10) — fit to LegendsOS DNA
7. RISK NOTES — every compliance or claim flag, specific
8. FIXES BEFORE PUBLISHING — numbered, ranked
9. FINAL APPROVED PROMPT — the corrected, paste-ready image prompt
```

Hard gate: Compliance Score under 8 means the image does not publish. Output a compliance review note instead.

```
# LegendsOS Visual Intelligence

## Core rule
AI builds the cinematic plate. Canva adds the words and compliance. Never have the image model render headlines, CTAs, logos, or legal text. Reserve a clean text zone and a bottom compliance strip.

## Steps
1. Ask only for topic and platform if missing, then produce the full package.
2. Pick engine: Cinematic Authority (trust), Pattern Interrupt (scroll-stop, sparing, never name competitors), Educational Minimal (one idea).
3. Pick a layout. Apply DNA: charcoal/black, Loan Factory orange + champagne gold, cinematic realism, strong face, one big idea, mobile-first.
4. Output: (1) Engine+Layout, (2) AI image prompt with default negative prompt and empty text/compliance zones, (3) Canva build with headline/support/CTA/logo/compliance strip, (4) caption in Jeremy's voice with one CTA and a next step, (5) visual boardroom review with Attention/Compliance/Trust/Readability/Brand scores + risk notes + fixes, (6) final approved prompt.

## Compliance (hard gate)
No approval promises. No "lowest rate/fees" unless sourced and approved. No rate without APR. No guarantee dollar amount unless current offer + eligible states confirmed; never promote in Washington; always add T&C URL and "not a commitment to lend; subject to credit approval; rates/terms/fees subject to change." NMLS #320841 complete and correct; LO NMLS when promoted. No named/depicted competitors. No fake testimonials, no loan docs, no borrower PII. No government endorsement for VA/FHA/USDA. "Terms, eligibility, and program guidelines apply" for programs. Compliance score under 8 = do not approve, output a review note.

## Default negative prompt
garbled text, fake text, misspelled words, distorted letters, gibberish signage, watermark, real bank or competitor logos, trademarked logos, fake legal text, real loan documents, extra fingers, deformed hands, plastic skin, oversaturated, cartoon mascot, clip art, busy footer, white flyer background, low resolution, blurry face.

End every package with the next step.
```
