# Example 04 — Coaching Offer Design

## Input

> Critique this offer: a $2,500 "AI for Real Estate Agents" 4-week cohort for my partner agents.

## Output

**1. EXECUTIVE VERDICT**
Fix-then-ship. The offer is in a real market and Jeremy is one of the few people who can credibly deliver it. The price and the audience are both right. The product shape needs sharpening, the proof needs to land in the first week, and the post-cohort path needs to convert agents into deeper partnership, not end at "thanks for coming."

**2. BOARDROOM WORKFLOW SELECTED**
Workflow 4 — Coaching Offer Design. Strategy Council with Offer, Leverage, Simplicity leading.

**3. ADVISOR SEAT REVIEWS**
- **Offer Seat:** $2,500 for 4 weeks is a real number for an agent who is moving 6 to 12 deals a year. The promise has to be specific. "Save 10 hours a week and look smarter to clients" beats "learn AI."
- **Tribe Seat:** partner agents are the ideal launch audience. They already trust Jeremy. The lift to enroll is small. The lift to retain after cohort 1 is everything.
- **Leverage Seat:** the work in cohort 1 is heavy. Cohort 2 and 3 reuse 80% of it. Build with reuse in mind from day one. Template lessons, screen recordings, prompt libraries, GPT setups.
- **Simplicity Seat:** 4 weeks is manageable for Jeremy as a solo cohort. Do not promise 1-on-1 office hours unless you mean it. Group calls + Loom debriefs scale.
- **Behavior Seat:** the agent's real fear is being left behind. The hook is "you will not look slow to your clients." Lead with that.
- **Cash Seat:** $2,500 x 10 agents = $25k. Real money for cohort 1, especially if delivery is leveraged. The bigger play is the relationship lift, not the cohort revenue.
- **Compliance Seat:** call it education. Not legal, tax, real estate, or mortgage advice. Do not promise outcomes ("you'll close 2x more deals"). Treat any rate / pricing example as illustrative.

**4. POINTS OF AGREEMENT**
Right market, right audience, right price band. Build for reuse from day one. Lead with a fear/relief promise.

**5. POINTS OF CONFLICT**
Simplicity vs Offer: how much 1-on-1 is included. Resolution: zero 1-on-1 in cohort 1. Group calls + asynchronous office hours via a Loom queue. Revisit after cohort 2.

**6. HIDDEN RISKS**
- Tier 2 Yellow if cohort marketing is public.
- Watch for any borrower-data examples used in lessons. Scrub or redact.
- Don't accidentally position the cohort as legal/financial advice. Education only.

**7. BEST OPPORTUNITY**
The cohort is a Realtor partnership engine wearing a course's clothes. Every agent who completes cohort 1 gets a co-branded marketing kit (RESPA-safe value-first, both licenses shown). The cohort is the on-ramp to a deeper referral relationship.

**8. RECOMMENDED DECISION**
Fix-then-ship.

**9. PRIORITY FIXES (ranked)**
1. Rename the promise. Working title: "AI for Real Estate Agents: 4 weeks to a sharper, faster, smarter practice." Lead with hours saved and client perception.
2. Cohort 1 deliverables (lock these): weekly group call, weekly Loom recap, prompt library (10 prompts per lesson), 2 plug-and-play GPTs by week 4, a 30-minute office-hour Loom queue.
3. Define the proof event in week 1: every agent ships one AI-built listing kit by end of week 1. Early win = retention.
4. Post-cohort path: co-branded buyer education flyer (RESPA-safe), invitation to a quarterly "agent roundtable," priority loan officer access via Jeremy. The cohort ends, the relationship doesn't.
5. Compliance line on marketing: "Educational. Not legal, tax, real estate, or mortgage advice. Results vary."

**10. FINAL ACTION PLAN**
- Today: pick the 10 launch agents from the partner list. DM with the new positioning.
- This week: lock the week-by-week curriculum and the proof event for week 1.
- Pre-launch: build the landing page (route through `companions/05-seo-aeo-geo-gbp`), the launch email sequence (route through `companions/04-email-newsletter`), and the social posts (route through `companions/06-social-media`).
- Launch: cohort 1 with 8 to 12 agents. Cap it. Scarcity is real here.
