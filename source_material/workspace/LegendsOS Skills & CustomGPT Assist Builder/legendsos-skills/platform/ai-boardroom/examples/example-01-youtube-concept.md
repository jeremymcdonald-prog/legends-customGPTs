# Example 01 — Viral YouTube Concept Review

## Input

> Boardroom this: a YouTube video called "I called 10 banks and 1 broker for the same loan." Worth making?

## Output

**1. EXECUTIVE VERDICT**
Fix-then-ship. The concept has real upside but the title implies a head-to-head comparison that has to be handled carefully on compliance. Sharpen the framing and the claims discipline and this is one of the strongest authority videos Jeremy could publish this quarter.

**2. BOARDROOM WORKFLOW SELECTED**
Workflow 1 — Viral YouTube Concept Review. Strategy Council with Compliance added because the topic is mortgage and the format invites rate / fee claims.

**3. ADVISOR SEAT REVIEWS**
- **Scale Seat:** the hook is built to travel. "10 banks vs 1 broker" is a curiosity gap with a punchy number. Survives a thumbnail at 240px.
- **Tribe Seat:** speaks directly to the buyer who has already gotten a bank quote and isn't sure what to do next. Specific audience, remarkable angle.
- **Offer Seat:** the implicit offer ("call a broker") needs to be the explicit CTA at the close. Right now the concept is curiosity, not conversion.
- **Behavior Seat:** buyer fear here is being taken advantage of by a bank. Lean into that. Pair the reveal with a 30-second "what to ask any lender" download as a comment-gate.
- **Compliance Seat:** if you cite real rate quotes from real banks, you cannot publish them without APR, assumptions, and "subject to change." Treat anything captured on a phone call as illustrative unless you have written permission. Do not name a specific competitor in the title or thumbnail.
- **Simplicity Seat:** this is a one-day shoot. Calls happen anyway as part of business. Capture is already free. Edit cost is real but bounded.

**4. POINTS OF AGREEMENT**
The angle is strong, the audience is sharp, the format is repeatable. Scale and Tribe agree this is a category Jeremy should own.

**5. POINTS OF CONFLICT**
Compliance vs Scale: the bigger the claim in the title, the bigger the disclaimer the video needs. Resolution: keep the title curiosity-driven, deliver the proof in the body with proper APR / assumption framing, and never compare specific bank names.

**6. HIDDEN RISKS**
- Tier 2 Yellow (public-facing).
- R4 triggered: unapproved content drift if specific rates show on screen without APR context.
- R10 not applicable (no auto-send).

**7. BEST OPPORTUNITY**
Run this as a series. "I called 10 lenders for [VA / FHA / DSCR / refi]" lets the same shot list and editorial template generate 4 to 6 videos. Authority compounds.

**8. RECOMMENDED DECISION**
Fix-then-ship.

**9. PRIORITY FIXES (ranked)**
1. Rewrite the title to remove the implied head-to-head: "I called 10 lenders for the same loan. Here's what I learned." Keeps the curiosity, drops the legal exposure.
2. Add a written intro card on screen: "Rates and quotes shown are illustrative. APR and terms vary by borrower. Not a commitment to lend."
3. Mask or omit specific bank names in audio and on screen. Use "the big retail lender," "the bank," "the broker," etc.
4. Tag the CTA at the close: comment "QUESTIONS" for the "what to ask any lender" PDF.
5. Add the compliance strip and NMLS footer to the thumbnail-adjacent description block.

**10. FINAL ACTION PLAN**
- Today: rewrite the title and shot list with the fixes above.
- This week: capture the calls; document permission status for each.
- Next: edit, add the on-screen disclaimer card, route the finished cut back here for an Asset Gate review before publishing.

Let me know what you'd like me to do next.
