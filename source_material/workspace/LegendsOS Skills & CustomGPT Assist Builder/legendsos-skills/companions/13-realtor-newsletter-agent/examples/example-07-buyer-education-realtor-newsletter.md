## Newsletter Body

A buyer who is fully prepared before the first showing closes at a different rate than a buyer who is shopping homes and shopping financing at the same time. The agents in our network who consistently close are the ones who treat the first conversation like a structure meeting, not a tour preview.

### Quick Market Context

In Northeast Florida, the most expensive thing a buyer can do in 2026 is start touring before structure is set. They fall in love with a house that does not match their program. Insurance comes in higher than expected. A debt shows up that nobody caught. The deal either dies or grinds. Either way the Realtor loses two weeks of momentum.

### Mortgage Strategy

Here are the three conversations every buyer should have before they walk into the first showing. This is pulled from the structure guide we publish at mcdonald-mtg.com.

**1. Payment, not price.**
Ask them what monthly payment they are comfortable with at the high end and the low end. Two numbers. Target payment and hard ceiling. Then we work the math backward into price, taxes, and insurance. Most buyers shop by list price and get blindsided by PITIA plus community costs.

**2. Documents on file, not promised.**
A buyer who says, "I will send you my pay stubs later," is not pre-approved. A buyer with documents in the file, credit pulled, and structure run is. Before the first showing, the buyer should know what their documents checklist looks like and where to upload it.

**3. Hidden debt and credit blind spots.**
The invisible debts that kill pre-approval are usually not the obvious ones. They are the buy now pay later balances, the small revolving cards, the cosigned auto loan, the medical collection that just appeared. Every one of these can move qualifying numbers. We catch them in the structure meeting, not in escrow.

### Deal Save Lesson

The buyer who is fully pre-approved before shopping is a different buyer than the buyer who is pre-qualified with a quick credit pull. Same word on paper. Different experience in the contract. The agents who teach their buyers the difference are the agents who hear "yes" faster.

Results vary based on individual circumstances.

### Realtor Talking Point

You can use this in your first call.

"Before we tour, I want to make sure your mortgage piece is locked into structure, not just a quick number. I work closely with a broker who runs full pre-approval, not just a credit check. It is a 20 minute conversation that protects every house we look at after this. Are you open to that step first?"

That sentence resets the buyer from "just looking" to "ready."
