## Newsletter Body

A buyer who feels confident in the mortgage piece tours like a buyer ready to buy. A buyer who is unsure tours like a buyer who needs to think about it. The difference between those two is not the market, it is the conversation they had before they walked into the house.

### AI Tip of the Week

Take five minutes this week and write a short note to every buyer in your pipeline that says, "Here is what I am seeing in your specific neighborhood right now and here is how my mortgage partner thinks about it." Local context plus a partner the buyer trusts wakes up cold pipeline faster than any new lead source.

### Mortgage Strategy Angle for Northeast Florida

Three things buyers in Jacksonville, St. Augustine, Nocatee, and Ponte Vedra need to know before they tour.

1. **Insurance and taxes drive payment, not just rate.** In Northeast Florida the property tax line and the wind insurance line can move a monthly payment by hundreds of dollars between two houses on the same street. A buyer who only ran a payment based on rate and price is shopping with the wrong number. We model roof age, wind mitigation, taxes, and insurance up front.
2. **Pre-approval is structure, not a number.** A real pre-approval covers the program fit, the down payment plan, the reserves, and the offer strategy. Rate is one line item. Structure is the whole deal. That is the framework I publish in our first time buyer structure guide on mcdonald-mtg.com.
3. **Sub-market matters more than the headline.** St. Johns County is moving differently than Riverside, which is moving differently than Mandarin, which is moving differently than the Beaches. A buyer who reads a national rate headline and a Realtor who knows the local pulse can be looking at two different markets in the same week.

### How This Helps Realtors Win

When you can frame the mortgage piece with this kind of local context, you stop sounding like an agent doing a tour and start sounding like a strategist. Buyers commit faster when they feel guided, not sold.
