## Newsletter Body

The agents who close consistently in Northeast Florida understand something that most agents only learn the hard way. A bank is not the same as a broker. They have different incentives, different menus, and different ways of handling your buyer once the contract is signed. If you treat them as interchangeable, you are quietly handing some of your deals to whoever is willing to fight harder for them.

### Quick Market Context

Buyers in 2026 are payment sensitive. They are taxing every line item. They are reading every cost. In that climate, a 200 dollar swing in monthly payment is the difference between an accepted offer and a polite "let me think about it." The shop the buyer's mortgage gets through is the shop that controls that swing.

### Mortgage Strategy

Here is the difference, written so a buyer could read it.

**A bank or retail lender** has one shelf. They sell their own loans. Their loan officer's job is to fit your buyer onto that shelf. If your buyer fits, great. If they do not, the deal stalls, or the buyer is told what they "qualify for" without ever knowing what else was possible.

**A mortgage broker** shops a wholesale marketplace. We are not selling one product. We are matching your buyer to the right structure across many lenders. Verbatim from our website. "I shop 90-230+ wholesale lenders instead of one bank menu." Same buyer. Same credit. Same income. Different result.

Three differences that show up in the contract.

1. **Lender fees.** Most banks stack 2,500 dollars or more in lender costs. Our team charges 0 dollars in lender fees.
2. **Program range.** We run Conventional, FHA, VA, USDA, Jumbo, DSCR, Fix and Flip, FHA 203k, HomeReady, Home Possible, ITIN, Bank Statement, and HELOCs for primary and investment. Banks rarely run half of that.
3. **Direct LO access.** You and your buyer work with me directly from strategy through closing. Not an 800 number. Not a different rep every week.

### Deal Save Lesson

The typical pattern. Buyer goes to their bank. Gets a quick number. Tours homes. Falls in love. Goes back to the bank. The bank's underwriting catches a structure issue the original quote missed. The deal either dies or grinds. The agent loses two weeks of momentum.

When the same buyer runs structure with a broker first, that issue is caught in week one. The deal is built on the right program from the start. The closing actually happens.

Results vary based on individual circumstances.

### Realtor Talking Point

You can use this with any buyer who is leaning toward their bank.

"You are welcome to go through your bank. I just want to make sure we get a second look from a broker who shops the market. If your bank wins the comparison, we move forward with confidence. If they do not, you just protected yourself from a structure you would not have known to ask about."

That sentence respects the buyer and protects the deal at the same time.
