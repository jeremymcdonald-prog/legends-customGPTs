## Newsletter Body

The investor buyer segment in Northeast Florida is more active than most consumer Realtors realize. Mandarin, Riverside, parts of the Westside, and pockets near the Beaches are quietly absorbing buy and hold investors who are running the math on rent versus payment and pulling the trigger. If you are equipped to talk to that buyer, you have less competition than you think.

### Investor Buyer Opportunity

The investor buyer is not the same as the consumer buyer. They do not ask, "Can I afford this." They ask, "Will this property carry itself." A Realtor who understands that question is the Realtor who keeps them. A Realtor who only quotes price and walks them through a tour loses them within two showings.

Our investor positioning at the team is straightforward, pulled directly from our Investor Hub. Investor focused mortgage solutions. Clear structure. Real numbers. No guessing.

### Loan Strategy

The two most common investor paths I see right now in Northeast Florida are buy and hold and short term renovation projects.

**DSCR (buy and hold).**
A DSCR loan qualifies the property on the income it produces, not just the borrower's personal income. That changes the conversation. Instead of running a full W2 and tax return workup, we run a rent versus payment analysis on the property itself. That is the structure DSCR is built around. I am not going to quote ratios or rates in this email because every deal is different. The point is the program exists, it is alive in our menu, and it is one of the cleanest tools for an investor who already has personal complexity in their tax returns.

**Bank statement and self employed.**
For investors who own a business, run multiple LLCs, or otherwise do not have a clean W2 picture, bank statement programs and self employed structures are tools that open doors a bank cannot. Same caution. Documentation requirements vary. Each one is reviewed individually.

### What Realtors Should Look For

**Green lights.**
- Buyer has a target neighborhood already, not "anywhere in Florida."
- Buyer has reserves and is comfortable talking about them.
- Buyer asks about rent comps before asking about list price.
- Buyer is building a portfolio, not buying a single dream home.

**Red flags.**
- Buyer cannot articulate their strategy beyond "I heard Jacksonville is good."
- Buyer wants the full retail consumer experience but expects investor pricing.
- Buyer is mixing consumer mortgage thinking with investor mortgage thinking. Those are two different lanes.

### What Buyers Need Ready

General preparation, no specifics without compliance review.

- A clear entity structure if buying in an LLC.
- Bank statements for the relevant period.
- Reserves verified, not just stated.
- A target market and a rent comp set for that market.
- A real estate partner who is comfortable running rent comp analysis.
