## Newsletter Body

The hardest part of working with first time buyers is not finding them. It is keeping their expectations and their qualification in the same conversation. If you can do that before the first showing, you stop spending Saturdays driving buyers through houses they cannot finance.

### Quick Market Context

First time buyers in Florida are walking into the market with more access to information than ever and more conflicting information than ever. They have seen too many TikToks. They have read too many headlines. They have looked at Zillow numbers that are not their numbers. The Realtor who sits them down and explains structure early is the Realtor they trust for the next ten years.

### Mortgage Strategy

The framework I use, pulled directly from the first time buyer structure guide we publish at mcdonald-mtg.com.

**1. Two numbers, not one.**
Ask the buyer for their target payment and their hard ceiling payment. Two numbers. The target is the payment they want to live with. The ceiling is the highest they will let themselves get pushed to in a competitive offer. Every conversation about price, taxes, insurance, and HOA gets run through those two numbers from this point forward.

**2. Rate is one line item. Structure is the whole deal.**
This is the most important sentence I can give a first time buyer. Rate is one variable in a six variable equation. Down payment, program fit, reserves, debt structure, payment shock, and offer strategy are all moving at the same time. The buyer who understands that does not panic on a rate headline.

**3. Pre-approval is a meeting, not a calculator.**
A real first time buyer pre-approval is a 20 to 30 minute conversation, with documents, with credit, and with structure. It is not a quick number from a website. The buyer who has done that work walks into showings differently than the buyer who has not.

**4. The right program for credit and timeline.**
Conventional, FHA, VA, USDA, HomeReady, Home Possible, ITIN, and other programs each have different doors that open or close based on credit, timeline, and reserves. A buyer who only knows about one of those is shopping in a small room.

### Deal Save Lesson

The first time buyer most likely to fall out of the deal is the one who got attached to a house before they understood their payment ceiling. The first time buyer most likely to close is the one whose Realtor and broker partnered early to set the two numbers, the program, and the offer strategy. Same buyer. Different prep work. Different outcome.

Results vary based on individual circumstances.

### Realtor Talking Point

You can use this on your next first time buyer intake call.

"I want to help you find the right home, but I want to do it the way that actually works. Before we tour anything, can we set up a 20 minute structure conversation with my broker partner. We will get you a real pre-approval, set a target payment, set a ceiling, and talk through the right program. Then every house we look at after that is a house you can actually buy."

That sentence converts more first time buyer leads into closed deals than any showing tactic.
