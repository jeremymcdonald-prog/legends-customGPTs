## Newsletter Body

Northeast Florida has one of the deepest pools of VA eligible buyers in the state. NAS Jacksonville. Mayport. Camp Blanding. Active duty, veterans, and military families are a major segment of every Jacksonville buyer pipeline. The Realtors who handle this segment well close consistently. The Realtors who default to old VA loan myths lose them.

### Quick Market Context

VA buyers have shifted the way they shop in 2026. They are more educated, more cautious, and more aware of how poorly some retail lenders handle their files. They are also more competitive than the average listing agent realizes. A well structured VA pre-approval, run through a broker who actually knows the program, can compete directly with conventional offers in most price ranges.

### Mortgage Strategy

Three things every Realtor representing a VA buyer should be working from.

**1. The pre-approval is structure, not paperwork.**
A VA buyer with a real pre-approval has had their entitlement reviewed, their residual income tested, and their reserves verified. A buyer with a "VA quote" from a 1-800 lender does not have that. The structure work is the difference between a clean closing and a 90 day grind.

**2. Listing agent objections to VA can be diffused.**
The biggest objections you will hear are old. "VA loans are slow." "VA appraisals are tough." "VA buyers cannot pay closing costs." None of those are accurate to today's lending environment in the hands of a good broker. We close VA files routinely with full structure, clean appraisals, and credible terms. Your buyer is competitive if the listing agent is given confidence in the file.

**3. The military move pattern is different.**
PCS timing, deployment overlap, and dual military families have specific qualifying nuances that most retail lenders treat as friction. We treat them as routine. We publish a full read at mcdonald-mtg.com under "Military PCS: The Best Spots Near NAS Jax, Mayport, and Camp Blanding (2026)."

### Deal Save Lesson

The most common pattern I see is a VA buyer who has been told by a retail lender that they "qualify for X." Then their Realtor writes an offer. Then underwriting starts asking for documents that should have been collected up front. The deal drags. The seller gets nervous. The buyer loses faith. Every one of those issues is avoidable when the broker pre-approval is run with structure, not speed.

Results vary based on individual circumstances.

### Realtor Talking Point

You can use this with any listing agent who questions a VA offer.

"My buyer is fully pre-approved through a broker, not a retail call center. Their entitlement is confirmed, their residual income is verified, and their reserves are in the file. The broker shops 90 plus wholesale lenders. This is a clean VA package."

That sentence wins more multiple offer situations than most agents realize.
