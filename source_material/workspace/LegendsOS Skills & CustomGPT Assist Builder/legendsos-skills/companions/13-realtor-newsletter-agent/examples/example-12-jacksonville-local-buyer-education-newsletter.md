## Newsletter Body

When a buyer asks me, "What is the rate today," I know they are still shopping the wrong thing. Jacksonville has a few specific quirks that change the way a mortgage gets structured here, and the Realtors who can articulate that to their buyers run a tighter, smarter pipeline.

### Quick Market Context

Jacksonville and Northeast Florida are not Miami. They are not Orlando. They are not Tampa. Buyers in Duval, Clay, and St. Johns County deal with a specific stack of decisions that locals know cold and out of state buyers do not. The Realtor who explains that stack early earns trust faster than the agent who only quotes price per square foot.

### Mortgage Strategy

Five things that make Jacksonville different on the financing side.

**1. Insurance and roof age math.**
Wind insurance is real money here. So is roof age. A house in the same neighborhood with a 17 year old roof versus a 4 year old roof can swing the buyer's monthly payment significantly. Buyers do not know to ask. Realtors who do, win.

**2. Sub-market by sub-market reality.**
St. Johns County is moving differently than Riverside. Riverside is moving differently than Mandarin. Mandarin is moving differently than Orange Park, Fleming Island, or Jacksonville Beach. A buyer reading a Florida headline is reading the wrong story.

**3. Military and VA presence.**
NAS Jacksonville, Mayport, and the broader military presence mean VA loans are a routine part of the market here, not a fringe program. Buyer agents who know how to talk VA fluently keep their military buyers from defaulting to a retail lender.

**4. New construction and the builder's lender trap.**
There is a lot of new construction in Northeast Florida. The builder almost always has a preferred lender with incentives attached. Those incentives are real, but they are usually packaged with structure that benefits the builder more than the buyer. We publish a full write up on this at mcdonald-mtg.com under "Why the Builder's Lender Isn't Always Your Friend."

**5. First time buyer programs in Florida.**
Florida has a stack of first time buyer programs that change the down payment math. Buyers in Duval and Clay County in particular ask about these all the time. A Realtor who knows which programs are worth running and which to skip can give the buyer a real number, not a generic answer.

### Deal Save Lesson

The Northeast Florida buyer most likely to break the deal is the one who only ran a rate and price calculation. The buyer who ran payment plus taxes plus insurance plus HOA, and got the structure right with a broker, is the buyer who tours like they are ready to buy.
