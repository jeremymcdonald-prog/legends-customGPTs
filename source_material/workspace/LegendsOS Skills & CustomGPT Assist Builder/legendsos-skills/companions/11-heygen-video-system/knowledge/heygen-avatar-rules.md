# HeyGen Avatar Rules

## When HeyGen is the right tool

- Bulk education plays where the message is the asset.
- Recurring program explainers (FHA, VA, USDA, DSCR, FTHB).
- Batch production weeks (Jeremy is on the road or in heavy ops).
- Realtor AI tip series (can run weekly).
- Weekly recap videos.
- Long form chapters where Jeremy on camera all 9 minutes is impractical.

## When Jeremy on camera beats HeyGen

- Personal stories (closing day reaction, family, gym, faith).
- Market reactions where humanity beats polish.
- Leadership moments (team announcements, CTO positioning).
- Anything where the audience needs to see the real human energy.

## Avatar selection

- Jeremy's primary avatar — default for all mortgage content.
- Persona selection (Inside Man / Coach / Wolf) drives delivery, not avatar identity.
- Other people's avatars require written authorization on file.

## Audio direction

- Conversational delivery.
- Pauses on key terms.
- Energy matched to persona.
- No artificial enthusiasm.

## Visual direction

- Premium dark luxury background per Visual DNA.
- Subject centered or slightly off-center.
- Orange or gold accent on one key element of the frame.
- Lower-third name plate when first introduced in a long form.
- Logo lockup top corner.

## Captions

- Always on. HeyGen-generated captions allowed but proofed.
- One key word emphasized in orange or gold.
- No emojis in captions.

## Music

- Subtle, never overpowers voice.
- Genre defaults to cinematic neutral or warm guitar/piano for stories.
- Higher tempo for Coach persona, lower for Inside Man.
- No royalty-uncleared tracks.

## End screens

- Short: single CTA card 2 to 3 seconds.
- Long: 2 CTA cards (subscribe + next video) 8 to 12 seconds, branded background.
