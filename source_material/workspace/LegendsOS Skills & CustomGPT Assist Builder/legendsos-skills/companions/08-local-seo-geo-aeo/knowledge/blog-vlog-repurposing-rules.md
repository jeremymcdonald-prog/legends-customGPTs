# Blog and Vlog Repurposing Rules

The blog and vlog are the canonical sources. Social derives from them.

## One blog post → 6 to 10 social derivatives

| Blog post asset | Derivative |
|---|---|
| Headline | Hook for Reel/Short/TikTok |
| First paragraph (direct answer) | IG Feed caption + LinkedIn opener |
| One H2 takeaway | One IG carousel slide |
| Pull-quote | IG Story frame + LinkedIn quote post |
| Bullet list | Carousel structure |
| Closing CTA | GBP post CTA + Reel/Short CTA |

## One vlog → 4 to 8 social derivatives

| Vlog asset | Derivative |
|---|---|
| Full video | YouTube Long Form publish |
| Best 30 seconds | Reel + TikTok + YT Short |
| Best quote | LinkedIn quote post |
| Transcript section | Blog supporting article |
| Thumbnail | Reel/Short cover |

## Sequencing

1. Blog or vlog publishes Tuesday or Thursday.
2. Reel/Short derivative same day.
3. GBP post and LinkedIn post Wednesday or Friday referencing the long form.
4. Story repost following weekend.
5. Email newsletter mention next available send.

## Voice consistency

The derivative copy never overpromises the original. If the blog post is teaching, the social derivative is teaching, not selling.

## Attribution

Every derivative links back to the long form when the platform supports a link. When it does not (Instagram caption), the CTA references "the full breakdown is on the site."

## Story pull

A blog post can be sourced from a Story Bank source ID. When it is, the post records the source ID in a hidden comment for tracking; the publish version uses the anonymized lesson summary, not the raw source.
