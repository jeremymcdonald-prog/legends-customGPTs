# Review-Informed Local Content

How to turn the patterns in Jeremy's public reviews into local-search content without ever inventing or misrepresenting a review.

## Themes that consistently appear in reviews (use as content angles)

- Communication speed and clarity.
- Closing on time even when other lenders had slipped.
- Broker access to lenders the borrower had not considered.
- VA loan experience and Veteran trust.
- First time buyer education and patience.
- Realtor partner respect ("Jeremy made me look good in front of my client").

## How to turn a theme into local content

1. Pick a theme that recurs across multiple reviews.
2. Write a local piece (GBP, blog, AEO block) that teaches the underlying mortgage lesson behind the theme.
3. Cite the theme generically: "Borrowers in Jacksonville keep telling us the difference is the communication on a Friday afternoon."
4. Do NOT name a specific reviewer unless the post is a verified-public-source reframe.
5. Do NOT pair the theme with a rate or savings claim.

## When you need to actually cite a review

- Source URL must be set (Zillow profile, Google profile, specific review URL).
- Citation reads "Public review on [platform]."
- Reviewer's published first name only.
- Reframe is still preferred over word-for-word, even when allowed.

## What this skill never does with reviews

- Aggregate reviews into a single "wall of stars" image without owner approval and verified source citations.
- Inflate the star count or volume.
- Translate a review's content into a guarantee or savings claim.
- Pair a review's reframe with a specific rate.

## When a review names a competitor

- Strip the competitor name.
- Replace with "a national lender" or "another broker."
- Keep the lesson; lose the call-out.

## Linking review content to local pages

- Review-themed content on a service page links to the GBP profile and the canonical review platform.
- Reviewer photos are not used unless the reviewer published them publicly AND we have a documented re-use right.
