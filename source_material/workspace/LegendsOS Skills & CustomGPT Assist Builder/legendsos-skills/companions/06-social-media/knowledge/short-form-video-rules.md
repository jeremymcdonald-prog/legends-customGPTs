# Short Form Video Rules

Applies to Instagram Reels, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts. Long form lives in the HeyGen Video System.

## Length

- Target 25 to 60 seconds.
- Hard cap 90 seconds.
- Under 30 seconds for pure pattern-interrupt or one-line takes.

## Structure

1. **Hook (0 to 2 seconds)** — visual + verbal. State the payoff.
2. **Setup (2 to 8 seconds)** — one sentence on the problem or context.
3. **Body (8 to 45 seconds)** — the teaching, the story, or the contrast.
4. **Land (last 5 seconds)** — the takeaway.
5. **CTA (last 2 seconds)** — one call. Save, share, comment keyword, follow.

## On-screen text

- 3 to 5 short lines total.
- Largest text reserved for the hook.
- One key word in orange or gold per line maximum.
- Compliance footer NOT on the video face unless rate or guarantee piece.

## Voice on camera

- Look in lens.
- First 2 seconds: stop scrolling, no warmup.
- Cut filler ("um, uh, so, basically").
- One idea per cut.

## When to use HeyGen vs Jeremy on camera

- Jeremy on camera: personal stories, market reactions, leadership moments, anything where humanity beats polish.
- HeyGen avatar: bulk education plays, AI tips, recurring program explainers, batch production weeks when Jeremy cannot film.

## When to use Higgsfield asset

- Hooks where a cinematic still or motion piece beats a talking head.
- Pattern-interrupt visuals.
- Lifestyle Northeast Florida B-roll style stills.

## Repurposing rule

Same script + on-screen text package can ship across Reels, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts on the same day, but the captions and CTAs vary per `caption-rules.md`.

## Music

- License-cleared trending audio on Reels and TikTok where appropriate.
- Original or generic on YouTube Shorts to keep monetization clean.
- Music never overpowers voice.

## What not to do

- No vertical subtitles that cover Jeremy's face.
- No three-stack text walls.
- No clickbait that the payoff doesn't deliver.
- No fake "I almost lost this deal" hooks unless a real Story Bank source backs it.
