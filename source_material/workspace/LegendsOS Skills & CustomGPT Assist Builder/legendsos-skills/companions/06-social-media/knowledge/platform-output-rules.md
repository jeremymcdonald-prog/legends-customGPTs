# Platform Output Rules

Per-platform formatting rules. These are the tactical guardrails the skill applies when writing each block.

## Facebook Personal

- Length: 80 to 220 words.
- Hook in the first line. No "I just want to share..." openers.
- No hashtags.
- One link max, only when relevant.
- Tone: personal, human.
- CTA: soft brand (Save this, Share with a buyer) or no CTA.
- Footer: only when mortgage-related.

## Facebook Business

- Length: 80 to 200 words.
- Hook line, then teach, then CTA.
- 0 to 3 hashtags max, branded only (#LegendsMortgage #LoanFactory #JacksonvilleMortgage).
- One link, often the website.
- Footer: required when mortgage-related.

## Instagram Feed

- Length: 90 to 180 words for single image; 150 to 250 for carousel.
- Strong hook, then short paragraphs separated by line breaks (no em dashes).
- 5 to 10 hashtags, mixed local + niche + branded.
- CTA: comment keyword or save.
- Carousel rule: hook on slide 1, one idea per slide, CTA on slide 8 to 10.
- Footer: required when mortgage-related.

## Instagram Reels

- Caption length: 60 to 140 words.
- Hook in caption AND in on-screen text on first second of video.
- On-screen text: 3 to 5 short lines max, big and readable on mobile.
- Hashtags: 5 to 10, blended.
- CTA: comment keyword or DM keyword from the safe CTA library.
- Footer: required when mortgage-related, in caption only, not on video unless it is a guarantee or rate piece.

## Instagram Stories

- 3 to 7 frames per story set.
- Frame 1: hook. Frame 2 to N: payoff. Last frame: CTA.
- Short text. No paragraphs. One thought per frame.
- Use poll, quiz, slider, question sticker where relevant.
- Footer: when mortgage-related, last frame includes NMLS + EHO mark.

## TikTok

- Caption length: 80 to 150 characters in the hook area; full caption can go longer.
- Hook in first 1.5 seconds of video AND in caption.
- On-screen text required.
- 3 to 5 hashtags. Less is more on TikTok.
- CTA: comment keyword or follow.
- Footer: mortgage pieces include the footer line in the caption.

## YouTube Shorts

- Caption length: 100 to 250 words. Front-load the hook.
- Title length: 40 to 70 characters.
- 3 to 5 hashtags in description, mortgage and AI tags.
- Thumbnail spec attached even though Shorts auto-pull; the skill outputs a thumbnail prompt anyway.
- CTA: subscribe or watch the long form.
- Footer: in description when mortgage-related.

## YouTube Long Form

- Title length: 50 to 70 characters. Front-load the keyword.
- Description: 250 to 600 words, with chapters and timestamps if available.
- 3 to 8 hashtags at end of description.
- Thumbnail spec: bold face, single big idea, orange or gold accent only, max 3 words on the thumbnail text.
- End screen: 1 CTA, 1 next video.
- Footer: in description when mortgage-related.

## LinkedIn

- Length: 80 to 250 words. Posts that read like a thought, not a brochure.
- No hashtag walls. 3 to 5 hashtags max.
- No link in the first comment trick unless the piece is built for it. If a link belongs, put it in.
- Tone: leadership, AI implementation, broker authority, candidate-for-CTO appropriate when relevant.
- CTA: comment-driven discussion, never "DM me."
- Footer: only when the post is mortgage-marketing facing.

## Google Business Profile

- Length: 80 to 250 words. Local angle required.
- One CTA button (Book, Learn More, Sign Up, Call).
- One image attached (Canva template).
- No keyword stuffing. Natural mention of city or county once or twice.
- Footer: NMLS + EHO mark in the body, not the image.

## Website blog

- Length: 600 to 1200 words.
- H1, H2 structure. AEO-ready Q and A blocks where natural.
- Internal links to 2 to 4 related pages.
- Schema: Article or FAQPage where relevant (see Local SEO skill).
- Footer: full NMLS + EHO + service area block.

## Website vlog

- Length: 200 to 400 word page with embedded video.
- Title doubles as the H1 and meta title.
- Transcript embedded for AEO.
- Schema: VideoObject.
- Footer: full NMLS + EHO + service area block.

## Cross-platform formatting hard rules

- Never use em dashes. Use commas, periods, ellipses.
- Never use emojis (Jeremy's preference; team adoption later).
- Never wrap a caption in quotes.
- Never use the phrase "Click the link in bio" alone; pair with a value reason.
- Never end a caption without a CTA on a marketing post.
