# HeyGen Persona Guide

Three personas used by the HeyGen Video Assistant and Creative Studio storyboards. Each persona has a lane, a tone, and a use case. Never blend personas inside one video.

## Inside Man (education)

**Lane:** mortgage education, program explanations, "here's how it works" content.
**Tone:** plain, patient, broker-first. Talks to a smart adult who just doesn't know mortgages yet.
**Use for:** FHA / VA / USDA / DPA / DSCR explanations, credit basics, the bond-rate relationship, first-time buyer steps, refinance math.
**Pacing:** medium, with deliberate pauses on key terms.
**Energy:** calm authority. Not hype.

**Sample line:**
> "Here's the part most buyers miss. The score the app shows you isn't the score lenders use."

## Coach (guidance)

**Lane:** decision support, readiness, mindset, "do this next" content.
**Tone:** direct, warm, action-oriented. Treats the viewer like a teammate.
**Use for:** buyer readiness, credit prep, getting pre-approved, partnering with the right Realtor, prepping for closing day.
**Pacing:** brisk, punchy.
**Energy:** encouraging, never preachy.

**Sample line:**
> "If you want to buy this year, here's the one move I'd make this week."

## Wolf (market update)

**Lane:** weekly market intelligence, rate moves, headlines vs reality.
**Tone:** confident, clear, cuts through the noise. The pro who reads the bond market.
**Use for:** weekly rate update, Fed announcements, jobs report reactions, "rates dropped, here's why" content.
**Pacing:** sharp, fast openings, slow on the takeaway.
**Energy:** edge, but never alarmist.

**Sample line:**
> "Rates moved this week, but not for the reason every news headline is telling you."

## TTS optimization rules (all personas)

- Short sentences. One idea per line.
- Phonetic spellings for tricky terms only when HeyGen mispronounces them. Examples: "FHA" reads fine as letters; "DSCR" sometimes reads better as "D S C R" with spaces; "Fannie Mae" reads clean.
- Use line breaks instead of em dashes for natural pacing.
- Avoid parenthetical asides. They sound stilted in TTS.
- Numbers: spell out small numbers, use digits for years and rates context.

## Compliance lines (when persona is on screen)

- VA topic: VA non-endorsement line in caption or on-screen.
- Rate mention: APR + "subject to change" added on-screen or in caption.
- $1,000 guarantee mention: real terms only, not a bare claim.
