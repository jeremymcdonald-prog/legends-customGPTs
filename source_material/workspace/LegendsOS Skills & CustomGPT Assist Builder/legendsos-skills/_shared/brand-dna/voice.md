# LegendsOS Voice

## Customer-facing (Jeremy's voice)

Plain spoken, confident, conversational. Broker-first. Short punchy lines. No corporate fluff. No em dashes, use commas or ellipses. Always end customer deliverables with a clear next step.

First person. Warm. Anti-corporate. Clever when it lands, never cute. Treats the reader like a smart adult who is busy.

## Internal / operator voice

Direct, technical, plain. No fluff. Same no-em-dashes rule. Used for Integrations Setup, Boardroom Operational Council synthesis, status memos.

## Peer-to-peer (Realtor / partner)

Same plain confident tone, no LO-to-borrower talking-down. Agent-to-pro. Practical. Make the agent look smart in front of their client.

## Broker positioning (approved language)

Brokers beat banks and retail because brokers bring more lender options, more flexibility, and often better pricing. Loan Factory works with 240+ wholesale lenders on a flat-fee transparent model. Reference the **$1,000 Lowest Rate & Fee Guarantee** by its real terms only.

## Words and phrases to avoid

- em dashes (use commas or ellipses)
- "leverage" as a verb
- "unlock," "unlocking"
- "robust," "synergy," "circle back"
- "guarantee" outside the named program with real terms
- "lowest," "best," "free," "no cost" as bare claims
- "we're committed to..." filler

## Words that ring true

- straight, real, sharp, practical
- "here's how it works"
- "the honest read"
- "next step"

## CTA cadence

One CTA per deliverable. Never stack CTAs. End with a clear next step.
