# Story Content Strategy

## Why stories carry the brand

Mortgage is a trust business. Stories are the trust currency. The Story Bank is the safe way to use stories without burning the relationship or the compliance posture.

## What "story" means here

- A real borrower experience, anonymized.
- A real Realtor scenario, anonymized.
- A real closing day, framed for the lesson.
- A real Zillow or Google review, reframed (or, when warranted, cited as a public review).
- A real moment from Jeremy's day, told without compliance friction.

## Story cadence in the 60-day plan

- Wednesday: story slot.
- Friday: story or closing day slot.
- Saturday: Realtor AI story (often lower compliance friction).

## Source ID seed plan

Before Week 1 locks, the Story Bank should hold at least these source records:

| Source type | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Zillow reviews intaken | 5 |
| Google reviews intaken | 5 |
| Borrower memories captured | 8 |
| Realtor scenarios captured | 4 |
| Website blog source links | 2 |

Total ≥ 24 source records, of which at least 12 are publish-ready (Yellow tier, gates closed).

## Risk discipline

- Yellow tier default.
- Red tier (named customer, rate quoted, guarantee) requires owner sign-off and slows the publish path.
- Green tier (no mortgage data, Realtor AI tips, personal brand) is the lightest path.

## Reframe over repost

Reposting a review word for word is rare. Default to the lesson reframe.

## The 11 frameworks (from Story Bank)

1. The File Save
2. The Veteran Win
3. The First Time Buyer Confidence Shift
4. The Realtor Rescue
5. The Payment Reality Check
6. The Broker Advantage Story
7. The Investor Numbers Story
8. The Move Up Buyer Strategy Story
9. The Appraisal or Inspection Curveball Story
10. The Closing Day Lesson
11. The AI Helped the Realtor Win Story

## Per-week framework target

- Minimum 4 different frameworks used.
- No single framework used more than twice in a week.
- VA + FTHB frameworks held for VA-pillar and FTHB-pillar days.

## Story → asset routing

Every published story carries the source ID in the internal production tracker (not in public copy). When the engine surfaces a top performer, it pulls the source ID back through the Story Bank to plan the next derivative.

## What this strategy refuses

- Invented customers.
- Invented reviews.
- Named customers without written permission.
- Property addresses without deal-public + approval.
- Rate or savings claims tied to a story without the proper disclaimer block.
