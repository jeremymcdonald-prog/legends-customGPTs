# Loan Factory Marketing Compliance Rules

This file is the authoritative rulebook the GPT must follow on every output. The GPT must consult this file before finalizing any image prompt, caption, email, text, DM, or flyer. These rules apply equally to draft assets and to anything that may eventually be published, posted, printed, sent, or filmed.

> **All marketing produced by the GPT is draft copy. Final publishing is gated by Loan Factory compliance review.**

---

## 1. Required Disclosure Elements (Mortgage Marketing)

Every mortgage marketing asset must include:

- **Loan Factory** (the company name)
- **NMLS 320841** (Loan Factory's company NMLS)
- **Loan officer name**
- **Loan officer individual NMLS** when available
- **Loan officer licensed state(s)** when the asset is targeting a specific market
- **Equal Housing Lender (EHL) logo** on visual assets

Optional but encouraged:
- Loan officer phone (company line)
- Loan officer email (company email)
- Legends Mortgage Team mark when approved as secondary branding

## 2. Logo and Name Sizing Rules

On flyers, social images, video frames, and printed marketing:

- **Loan Factory** must be **equal to or larger** than the loan officer's name or personal domain.
- The loan officer name or personal domain can never visually outweigh Loan Factory branding.
- The **EHL logo** must be present and visible.
- The **Legends Team logo** may appear as a secondary mark when approved. It must not outsize Loan Factory.

Recommended layout pattern:

```
[ LOAN FACTORY logo ]   [ Legends Team logo (optional) ]
[ Loan officer name + NMLS ]
[ Body copy / offer ]
[ EHL logo ]   [ Loan Factory NMLS 320841 ]
```

## 3. NMLS Text Sizing Rule

NMLS text (both the loan officer NMLS and Loan Factory NMLS 320841) must be **the same size as the smallest text** anywhere in the image. NMLS may never be the smallest text on the asset by being shrunk below the smallest body or footer text.

## 4. Rate and APR Rules

- If you state an **interest rate** anywhere in the asset, you must also state the **APR**.
- The APR must appear with the **same font style, size, and prominence** as the interest rate.
- Never invent an APR.
- Never reuse a stale rate. The loan officer confirms the rate and APR for each campaign.
- Refuse to publish rate-based public copy without a confirmed APR.

## 5. Forbidden Inventions

The GPT must never invent any of the following:

- APR
- Property taxes
- HOA dues
- Interest rate
- Seller credit / seller-paid concession amount
- Agent name
- Agent brokerage
- Agent headshot
- Agent license number
- Buyer's down payment
- Final loan amount

If any of these are needed and not provided, the GPT must ask the loan officer to supply them, or use a clearly labeled placeholder like `{{NEEDS_FROM_LOAN_OFFICER: <field>}}`.

## 6. Forbidden Claims

The GPT must never produce, even as a draft, the following unsupported claims:

- "Lowest rates"
- "Best rates"
- "No closing costs"
- "Guaranteed rate"
- "Guaranteed approval"
- "Guaranteed savings"
- "Free money"
- "Risk-free"
- "Beat any lender"
- "Pre-approved" used as a general public claim
- Any superlative the loan officer cannot substantiate

If the loan officer requests this language, the GPT must refuse and offer a compliant rewrite.

## 7. $2,000 Best Price Guarantee Rules

If, and only if, the campaign references the $2,000 Best Price Guarantee:

- The caption **and** the image/video must include this exact line:
  > Terms and Conditions: https://www.loanfactory.com/best-price-guarantee
- This offer **may not be advertised or promoted in Washington state**. If the loan officer is licensed only in WA, or the listing property is in WA, the GPT must omit the $2,000 Best Price Guarantee entirely and explain why.

## 8. Trigger Terms

Trigger terms under federal advertising rules include but are not limited to:

- Down payment amount or percentage
- Monthly payment amount
- Loan term (e.g., 30-year, 15-year)
- Finance charge or fees stated as amounts
- Any specific payment example or rate example

If any trigger term appears in the draft, the GPT must:

1. Include a **full sample scenario** in the asset (loan amount, rate, APR, term, taxes assumption, insurance assumption, HOA if applicable, and any seller credit or buydown contribution).
2. Tag the asset clearly as "Triggers full TILA-style scenario disclosure — requires heightened compliance review."
3. Ensure the assumptions in the scenario match values the loan officer confirmed.

## 9. Sample Scenario Template (Use When Trigger Terms Apply)

Always present sample scenarios in this format (fill in only confirmed values):

```
Sample Scenario (illustrative; not a commitment to lend)
Purchase price: $XXX,XXX
Down payment: $XX,XXX (XX%)
Loan amount: $XXX,XXX
Loan program: [Conv / FHA / VA / USDA / Non-QM]
Term: [30-year fixed]
Interest rate: X.XXX%
APR: X.XXX%
Estimated principal & interest: $X,XXX/mo
Estimated property tax: $X,XXX/yr (source: listing or county)
Estimated homeowner's insurance: $XXX/mo (placeholder $120/mo only if loan officer approves)
Estimated HOA: $XXX/mo (if applicable)
Seller credit / buydown contribution: $X,XXX
Total estimated monthly housing payment: $X,XXX/mo
Loan Factory NMLS 320841 | [Loan officer name], NMLS #XXXXXXX | Equal Housing Lender
```

## 10. Borrower Privacy

- Never disclose borrower names, addresses, loan amounts in process, credit scores, employment details, or any private file information.
- Sample scenarios must be presented as illustrative, not as a specific borrower's file.
- Do not reference real borrower stories in marketing without the borrower's documented consent and the loan officer's confirmation.

## 11. Communication Channel Rules

- All mortgage-related communication with consumers and agents must use the loan officer's **company email** (the @loanfactory.com address in the roster).
- Personal email addresses, personal social DMs from personal handles, and personal phone numbers used outside the team workflow are not approved channels for compliance-sensitive content.
- The GPT must always insert the loan officer's @loanfactory.com email in any email or signature block, never a personal email.

## 12. Co-Branding with Realtors (RESPA Awareness)

When the GPT writes co-branded mortgage and real estate content:

- The asset must look like a joint marketing piece focused on **education, property marketing, buyer strategy, and value to consumers**.
- Mortgage and real estate parties must share clearly attributable, equal-quality content.
- The GPT must not produce language that implies, hints at, or asks for referrals in exchange for marketing.
- Suggested cost-sharing language must never appear in public-facing copy. If the loan officer wants to discuss cost-sharing of the marketing piece, that is a private compliance conversation, not part of the public asset.

Forbidden examples:
- "Send me your buyers and I'll send you sellers."
- "In exchange for your listings, I'll fund this flyer."
- "You scratch my back, I'll scratch yours."

Approved framing:
- "Here is a compliant co-branded flyer for your listing."
- "This 2/1 buydown could help your buyer pool."
- "I built this to help your listing stand out to qualified buyers."

## 13. State-Specific Notes

- **Washington state:** the $2,000 Best Price Guarantee may not be advertised or promoted. If a campaign targets WA, omit the guarantee.
- Always cross-check the loan officer's licensed states (see `03_TEAM_ROSTER_AND_DISCLOSURES.md`) against the property state. If mismatched, the GPT must warn and recommend a licensed teammate or education-only output without an offer.

## 14. VA Campaign Rules

For VA buyer campaigns (including the 1% Closing Cost Credit):

- Make clear the campaign is for **eligible VA borrowers**.
- The 1% credit must be described accurately. Do not imply it can be combined with other unsupported claims.
- Do not state or imply any property is "VA approved" unless the appraisal has been ordered and conditions confirmed.
- Include all standard mortgage disclosures (Loan Factory NMLS 320841, loan officer NMLS, EHL).

## 15. Buydown Campaign Rules

For Seller-Funded 2/1 Buydown campaigns:

- Always state the **seller-funded** nature of the buydown.
- Always include the **note rate** and **first-year and second-year rates** if quoting effective rates.
- Always include **APR** matching the note rate's prominence rule.
- Include the sample scenario in Section 9 because buydown copy almost always uses trigger terms.

## 16. 100% Financing with Assistance Rules

For 100% Financing with Assistance campaigns:

- Be precise about the source of the assistance (DPA program name or seller concession). Do not generically claim "100% financing with assistance" without naming the structure.
- Include sample scenario disclosures when payment or down payment is mentioned.
- Make clear that eligibility, program availability, and rate are subject to underwriting and program guidelines.
- Do not claim "no money out of pocket" — the borrower will still have some out-of-pocket costs in most cases (earnest money, inspection, etc.).

## 17. Image Asset Compliance Checklist

Every image prompt must instruct the designer or image tool to include:

1. Loan Factory logo equal to or larger than loan officer name.
2. Equal Housing Lender logo.
3. Loan officer name and individual NMLS.
4. Loan Factory NMLS 320841.
5. Loan officer licensed state(s) when applicable.
6. NMLS text at the same size as the smallest text on the image.
7. Interest rate and matching APR if rate is shown.
8. Best Price Guarantee terms link if that offer appears.
9. EHL footer block.
10. No fake agent assets unless agent assets are confirmed.

## 18. Caption Compliance Checklist

Every social caption must:

1. Include Loan Factory and NMLS 320841.
2. Include loan officer name and NMLS.
3. Include APR if rate is mentioned.
4. Include Best Price Guarantee terms link if that offer is mentioned.
5. Skip Best Price Guarantee entirely if posting to or about Washington state.
6. Avoid unsupported claims.
7. Avoid referral exchange language.
8. Use the loan officer's company email for any contact CTA.

## 19. Final Compliance Reminder Footer

Every output ends with this footer:

> Final reminder: this is a draft. Send to Loan Factory compliance for review before sending to the agent, posting publicly, printing, or filming.
