## Why Hooks Are Everything

YouTube's algorithm judges videos in the first 30 seconds. If viewers click away in under 30 seconds, the algorithm stops recommending the video. If they stay past 30 seconds, engagement metrics improve and distribution increases.

The hook isn't the introduction. The hook is the entire bet. Get it wrong, and great content goes unseen.

For mortgage content, this matters even more than most niches, because the default viewer assumption is "this will be boring and try to sell me something." I'm fighting that assumption from the first frame.

---

## The Three Hook Mechanics That Drive Mortgage Content

**1. The Knowledge Gap**
Create a gap between what the viewer knows and what they need to know. The discomfort of the gap keeps them watching. Most effective setup: "Here's something [authority figure / lender / bank] doesn't want you to know."

**2. Stakes Escalation**
Make the cost of not knowing feel real and specific. Dollar amounts work better than percentages. "That's $16,000 over the life of your loan" hits harder than "that's 0.5% higher."

**3. Identity Activation**
Speak to who the viewer IS or wants to be. "If you're a veteran" or "If you're a first-time buyer" activates identity and signals relevance. Viewers who self-identify stay longer because the content feels personal.

## 15 Tested Hook Templates for Jeremy's Channel

### Hook 1: Bold Contrarian
**Template:** "[Conventional wisdom] is [wrong/backwards/costing you money]."  
**Mortgage version:** "Getting pre-approved at your bank before you find a house is one of the most expensive mistakes you can make."  
**Why it works:** Contradicts common advice from a position of authority. Creates immediate cognitive dissonance.

### Hook 2: Surprising Statistic
**Template:** "Most people don't know that [statistic]. That's [consequence]."  
**Mortgage version:** "Most people don't know that two borrowers with identical credit scores can get rates that are 0.625% apart. On a $350,000 loan, that's $16,000."  
**Why it works:** Concrete data makes abstract concepts feel real and urgent.

### Hook 3: Short Client Story
**Template:** "[Client situation] until [problem]. Here's what I found."  
**Mortgage version:** "Marcus called me two days before his bank closing. He'd been pre-approved at 7.25%. I got him 6.625% in 48 hours. Same loan, different lender. He almost left $14,000 on the table."  
**Why it works:** Narrative triggers empathy. Viewers project themselves onto the client.

### Hook 4: Stakes Escalator
**Template:** "If you don't know [thing], it will cost you [specific amount]."  
**Mortgage version:** "If you don't know the difference between APR and interest rate on your mortgage, it will cost you. I've seen buyers overpay $8,000 in fees because they compared the wrong numbers."  
**Why it works:** Fear of specific financial loss is a powerful motivator. The specificity makes it feel real.

### Hook 5: Insider Knowledge Reveal
**Template:** "Here's what they don't tell you about [topic]."  
**Mortgage version:** "Here's what your bank loan officer is never going to tell you about how mortgage pricing actually works. And it costs the average borrower $5,000."  
**Why it works:** "Insider" framing implies exclusive access. Creates a parasocial "I'm on your side" relationship.

### Hook 6: Myth Buster
**Template:** "You've probably been told [myth]. That's actually not true. Here's what is."  
**Mortgage version:** "You've probably been told you need 20% down to buy a house. I need to clear something up about that."  
**Why it works:** Directly contradicts a widely held belief, which creates the urge to resolve the cognitive dissonance.

### Hook 7: Short Contrarian Claim
**Template:** "[One-sentence contrarian claim.] I'll prove it in [X] minutes."  
**Mortgage version:** "Your credit score matters less than you think. I'll show you why in four minutes."  
**Why it works:** The time-bound promise reduces commitment friction. Easy to stay for 4 minutes.

### Hook 8: Comparison Setup
**Template:** "[Option A] vs. [Option B]. Most people choose wrong. Here's why."  
**Mortgage version:** "FHA vs. conventional. Most first-time buyers choose FHA automatically. Depending on your situation, that could be a $40,000 mistake."  
**Why it works:** Binary framing is cognitively easy. People want to know which side they're on.

### Hook 9: Warning Label
**Template:** "Before you [action], watch this. Because [consequence]."  
**Mortgage version:** "Before you accept your mortgage pre-approval, watch this. Because there's a number on that letter that most buyers never look at, and it costs them thousands."  
**Why it works:** Warning framing triggers caution response. Strong relevance for anyone actively in the process.

### Hook 10: Market Timing
**Template:** "Something is happening in the [market/rates/housing market] right now that most people are missing."  
**Mortgage version:** "Something is shifting in the Jacksonville housing market right now that I haven't seen since 2019. If you're planning to buy in the next 6 months, you need to know this."  
**Why it works:** Urgency + local relevance. Creates FOMO for the local audience.

### Hook 11: The Vulnerability Open
**Template:** "I made a mistake that I see buyers make all the time. Here's what it was."  
**Mortgage version:** "Early in my career, I almost cost a client $12,000 because I missed something on their loan estimate. I've never missed it since. Here's what I missed."  
**Why it works:** Vulnerability creates trust. It's unexpected from a financial professional.

### Hook 12: Identity Activation
**Template:** "If you're [specific identity], this is the most important thing I can tell you about [topic]."  
**Mortgage version:** "If you're a veteran or active duty military, this is the most important thing I can tell you about your VA loan benefit. Most service members leave $30,000 on the table."  
**Why it works:** Directly activates identity. Military audience has extremely high engagement when content is specifically addressed to them.

### Hook 13: Process Preview
**Template:** "In the next [time], I'm going to walk you through exactly how [process] works. Step by step."  
**Mortgage version:** "In the next eight minutes, I'm going to walk you through exactly how the mortgage process works from application to closing. Every step, every document, every timeline."  
**Why it works:** Process previews reduce anxiety. Homebuyers are often anxious about the unknown. This promises clarity.

### Hook 14: Unexpected Agreement
**Template:** "Everyone says [thing]. They're right. But they're missing the more important part."  
**Mortgage version:** "Everyone says 'shop around for your mortgage rate.' They're right. But they're all missing the part that matters more than the rate."  
**Why it works:** Agreement followed by escalation feels credible. Not contrarian for its own sake.

### Hook 15: Two-Part Promise
**Template:** "I'm going to tell you [thing 1] and [thing 2]. Stay for both."  
**Mortgage version:** "I'm going to tell you exactly what my $1,000 guarantee says and exactly what it doesn't say. Because the second part matters just as much."  
**Why it works:** Dual promise creates two reasons to stay. The "what it doesn't say" creates additional curiosity.
