# Epic Real Estate — Engagement Tactics

Engagement is not just vanity. YouTube's algorithm treats comments, likes, and watch time as quality signals. More engagement = more distribution. These are the tactics that drive it.

---

## How Epic RE Drives Engagement

**1. The Comment Question**
Every video ends with a specific question directed to the audience. Not "what do you think?" — that's too vague. Something like "Tell me in the comments: what's the one thing holding you back from buying an investment property right now?" Specific questions get specific answers, which drive comment volume.

**2. The "Us vs. Them" Frame**
Content framed around "here's what the banks/system don't want you to know" creates emotional investment. When viewers feel like they're getting inside information, they share it. Social sharing is the highest-value engagement signal.

**3. Community Identity**
The channel gives its audience an identity — people who are awake to how the system works and taking action to protect themselves. Belonging to a community is a powerful retention mechanism.

**4. Responding in Comments**
Actively responding to comments in the first 48 hours drives algorithmic favorability and creates a feedback loop that brings commenters back to check for replies.

**5. Pinned Comment**
Pinning a creator comment at the top of the thread (often with a resource link or a follow-up question) drives additional replies and keeps the comment section active longer.

## Engagement Tactics for Jeremy's Channel

**In-Video Tactics:**

- Ask one specific question at the end of every video — this single habit can 5-10x comment volume
- Use "watch to the end" verbal cues at the 20-30% mark — reduces drop-off and signals quality to YouTube
- Create "chapters" using timestamps — longer watch time per session, and chapters rank in Google Search
- Reference previous videos — drives session length as viewers go watch the referenced content
- Respond to every comment within 48 hours, especially in the first week after publishing

**Cross-Platform Tactics:**

- Tease the YouTube video on Instagram Stories the day before publishing — drives a first-wave traffic surge
- Share the video in relevant Facebook Groups for Northeast Florida buyers and military families
- LinkedIn posts with the video link perform for the professional/investor audience
- Email list gets a dedicated video notification with a personal note, not just a link

**The First 48 Hours Protocol:**
YouTube's algorithm evaluates each video's momentum in the first 24-48 hours. The engagement rate in that window determines how aggressively YouTube promotes it. Structure the launch around this window:
- Day -1: Tease on all social platforms
- Day 0 (publish): Immediately comment on your own video with a resource or follow-up question
- Day 0-1: Respond to every single comment within hours
- Day 1-2: Share in all relevant groups, email list
- Day 3: Check analytics; if CTR is below 3%, swap the thumbnail

---

## Engagement Traps to Avoid

**Buying engagement:** Purchased likes, comments, or subscribers tank your algorithm trust. YouTube detects unnatural patterns and suppresses the channel.

**Ignoring comments:** Not responding signals to active commenters that their input doesn't matter. They stop engaging — and their absence shows up in the algorithm.

**Clickbait without delivery:** If the thumbnail or title promises more than the video delivers, YouTube's own satisfaction survey (served to viewers after the video) will reflect it. Algorithmic suppression follows.

**Begging for likes:** "Please like and subscribe" in the first 30 seconds before you've delivered any value feels desperate and often backfires. Earn it first, then ask.
