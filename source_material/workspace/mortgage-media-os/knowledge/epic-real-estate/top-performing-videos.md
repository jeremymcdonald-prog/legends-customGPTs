# Epic Real Estate — Top Performing Video Categories

An analysis of the content types that drive the highest views, longest watch time, and strongest engagement for the Epic Real Estate channel — adapted for Jeremy's mortgage content strategy.

---

## High-Traffic Content Categories

### 1. Market Prediction Content
Titles and topics around "what's going to happen to home prices" consistently generate massive traffic. Why? Because everyone with a stake in the housing market (buyers, sellers, investors, homeowners) wants to know the answer. Nobody has it with certainty, but authoritative analysis attracts enormous search volume.

**For Jeremy:** "What's Really Happening With Jacksonville Home Prices in 2026" type content. Local angle makes it more searchable and more trustworthy.

### 2. "How to Get Started" Beginner Explainers
The single largest audience for real estate and mortgage content is people who are thinking about it for the first time. These viewers search with high urgency and low vocabulary. They need someone to speak their language.

**For Jeremy:** "How To Get Started Buying Your First Home in Northeast Florida" or "The Complete Beginner's Guide to Getting a Mortgage" — these are cornerstone content pieces that rank and compound for years.

### 3. Equity and Asset Protection Content
This is Epic RE's current sweet spot. Content about protecting what you've built — stopping hidden fees, avoiding probate traps, understanding when to refinance — speaks to homeowners' core anxiety in an uncertain market.

**For Jeremy:** "Is Your Mortgage Costing You More Than It Should?" and "How To Know If You Should Refinance Right Now" directly address this anxiety with actionable guidance.

### 4. Passive Income / Cash Flow Properties
Epic RE's legacy content around building cash flow through investment properties continues to drive views because the search intent never goes away.

**For Jeremy:** The investor content lane includes DSCR loans, the BRRRR strategy, and fix-and-flip financing. This is a secondary audience but a high-value one.

### 5. Myth-Busting Format
Videos structured as "You were told X — here's why X is wrong" consistently outperform straightforward explainers. The myth-busting format creates immediate tension and a clear payoff structure.

**For Jeremy:** "5 Mortgage Myths That Are Costing Buyers in 2026" — powerful format, high search volume, shareable by nature.

---

## What The Top Videos Have In Common

1. **Specific, promise-laden titles** — not "About VA Loans" but "How To Use Your VA Loan Twice — Even If You Think You Can't"
2. **A clear villain** — the bank, the lender, the system, the misinformation — something or someone the audience can root against
3. **A specific number** — "$40,000" or "68% of veterans" or "3 seconds" — specificity signals credibility
4. **A payoff** — the viewer leaves with something actionable, not just informed
5. **A community question** — drives comments and keeps the thread active
