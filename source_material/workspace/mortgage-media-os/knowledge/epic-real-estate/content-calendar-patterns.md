# Epic Real Estate — Content Calendar Patterns

Understanding how Epic RE structures their content calendar helps me build a system that is sustainable, algorithmically smart, and audience-friendly.

---

## Epic RE's Publishing Pattern

**Cadence:** Weekly, consistent. One anchor video per week.

**Format:** Show-based, not episodic one-offs. The channel runs "The All New Epic Real Estate Investing Show" as a named, recurring series. This is a critical distinction. Shows create audience habit. One-offs just create content.

**Podcast integration:** The podcast reinforces the YouTube content for audio audiences. Same ideas, different delivery. This content repurposing model is smart — it doubles reach without doubling production work.

**Best days for financial/mortgage content publishing (research-backed):**
- Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday perform strongest for educational finance content
- 9-11 AM ET is the peak search and viewing window for this category
- Avoid Fridays and weekends for long-form content (attention drops significantly)

## My Recommended Content Calendar Structure

### Weekly Anchor Video (Long-Form)
**Target length:** 8-15 minutes
**Format:** Educational explainer, market update, or client story
**Publishing day:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 9 AM ET
**Goal:** Build authority, drive search traffic, establish the show habit

### Shorts (3x per week)
**Target length:** 45-90 seconds
**Format:** Single tip, single question answered, hook from the long-form video
**Publishing days:** Monday, Wednesday, Friday
**Goal:** Algorithmic presence, discovery, feed the YouTube Shorts audience

### Monthly Deep Dive
**Target length:** 20-30 minutes
**Format:** Comprehensive guide (e.g., "Complete VA Loan Guide for Northeast Florida Buyers")
**Goal:** Cornerstone content that ranks in search for years

## Topic Rotation System

To avoid repeating the same topics too quickly while covering all of Jeremy's core areas, rotate through these content buckets:

**Week 1 — VA/Military**
- VA loan how it works, eligibility, myth-busting, specific scenarios

**Week 2 — First-Time Buyers**
- The process, what to expect, how to get ready, mistakes to avoid

**Week 3 — Rate Environment**
- Current rates, what they mean, when to lock, market update

**Week 4 — Broker Advantage**
- Why broker beats bank, the $1,000 guarantee, how to shop rates

**Rotate and repeat, adapting to current market conditions.**

## Evergreen vs. Timely Content Balance

**Evergreen content (60% of output):**
Topics that will get searches in 3 years, not just 3 weeks.
- "What is a VA loan and how does it work"
- "How to improve your credit score for a mortgage"
- "How much home can I afford"
- "What are closing costs"

These videos are the long-term asset. They compound over time.

**Timely content (40% of output):**
Topics tied to current events or market conditions.
- "What today's Fed decision means for your mortgage rate"
- "Should you buy a home in Jacksonville in 2026"
- "How the new NAR rules affect buyers this year"

These videos spike in the short term and drive new subscribers.

---

## The Content Machine Flywheel

1. **Long-form anchor video** covers the topic comprehensively
2. **Short-form clips** extract the three best 60-second moments
3. **Social captions** are written from the video transcript
4. **Email subject line** teases the video to the email list
5. **Comment question** at end of video drives community engagement
6. **Community response** generates next video topics

One good anchor video generates 2 weeks of social content. That is the efficiency model.
