## What 3.9 Months of Inventory Means

The classic definition:
- Below 4 months: Seller's market
- 4-6 months: Balanced market
- Above 6 months: Buyer's market

We just crossed from balanced into seller-friendly territory. This means:
- Multiple offers are returning on well-priced homes in desirable areas
- Homes sitting 60+ days still have negotiating room (something is wrong — price, condition, or location)
- Seller concession requests require more strategic framing than they did 6 months ago
- Pre-approval is non-negotiable before making an offer

## Key Submarkets in Northeast Florida

**Jacksonville Beaches (Jacksonville Beach, Neptune Beach, Atlantic Beach):**
Premium market. Inventory is tightest here. Median prices are higher ($400K-$600K+). Buyers need strong financial profiles and fast decision-making.

**Oakleaf / Orange Park / Fleming Island:**
Popular with families and first-time buyers. Good value relative to proximity to employment centers. Strong VA loan activity due to proximity to NAS Jacksonville.

**Nocatee / Ponte Vedra:**
Master-planned community. Premium pricing. Strong appreciation history. Less VA loan activity — price points exceed VA conforming limits for some buyers.

**Fernandina Beach / Nassau County:**
Coastal and growing. Strong equity appreciation. Active second-home and investor market.

**St. Johns County:**
Top-rated school district in Florida (A-rated consistently). High demand from families. Inventory extremely tight in entry-level price range.
