## What Every 20 Points Means to Your Rate

On a $350,000 loan (30-year fixed), here is what your credit score costs you:

- 760+: Best available rate (baseline)
- 700-759: +0.25%-0.50% (roughly $50-$100/month more)
- 660-699: +0.50%-0.75% (roughly $100-$150/month more)
- 620-659: +0.75%-1.25% (roughly $150-$250/month more)

A 100-point score improvement can save you $150-$250 per month on your mortgage. Over 30 years, that is $54,000-$90,000. The work to improve your score is worth it.

## The Five Fastest Ways to Move Your Score

**1. Dispute Errors on Your Credit Report**
Check all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion). One in four reports contains an error. Medical collections you don't recognize, accounts that aren't yours, outdated negative items — all disputable. A successful dispute can add 20-100+ points in 30 days.

**2. Pay Down High Credit Card Balances**
Credit utilization — how much of your available revolving credit you're using — is the second largest factor in your score (after payment history). Keep it below 30%, ideally below 10%. Paying a maxed-out $5,000 card to $1,500 (30% utilization) can add 30-50 points in one billing cycle.

**3. Rapid Rescore (Only Available Through Mortgage Lenders)**
This is a secret weapon. When I identify an error or a correctable item on your report, I can submit documentation to the credit bureaus through a rapid rescore process and get your score updated in 3-5 business days — not the 30-45 days a normal dispute takes. This is exclusively available through mortgage lenders, not directly to consumers.

**4. Become an Authorized User**
If a family member has an old credit card with a long history and low utilization, having them add you as an authorized user adds their positive history to your report. You don't even need to use the card. Impact can be 20-50 points in 30-60 days.

**5. Stop Applying for New Credit**
Every hard inquiry drops your score 5-10 points. Multiple hard inquiries in a short period signal risk to lenders. The exception: mortgage rate shopping. Multiple mortgage inquiries within a 14-45 day window typically count as one inquiry under FICO models.

## Credit Score Timelines — Realistic Benchmarks

- 500 → 580 (FHA minimum): 3-6 months of consistent work
- 580 → 620 (conventional minimum): 3-6 additional months
- 620 → 700 (solid conventional terms): 6-12 months
- 700 → 740 (best conventional pricing): 6-12 additional months

These are realistic timelines for someone actively working on their credit, not just paying bills on time and waiting.

---

## What NOT to Do When Preparing for a Mortgage

- Do not open new credit cards or loans (hard inquiry + reduces average account age)
- Do not close old credit cards (reduces available credit = increases utilization)
- Do not make large unexplained deposits or withdrawals (underwriting will ask)
- Do not change jobs if you can possibly avoid it (income verification resets)
- Do not co-sign anyone else's loan (adds their debt to your DTI)
