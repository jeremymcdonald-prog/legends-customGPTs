# DSCR Loans — Jeremy's Investor Guide

DSCR loans are the best thing to happen to real estate investors since the 1031 exchange. Here's why, and how they work.

---

## What Is a DSCR Loan

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio. It is a loan type where qualification is based entirely on the property's rental income versus its debt obligation — not on your W-2s, tax returns, or personal income.

The formula: **DSCR = Monthly Rental Income / Monthly PITIA (Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + Association dues)**

If the property generates $2,500/month in rent and the mortgage payment is $2,000, your DSCR is 1.25. That means the property covers its own debt with 25% to spare.

---

## Why Investors Love DSCR

The traditional investor loan requires you to qualify using your personal income, including all your other properties. The more properties you own, the harder it gets to qualify for the next one — even when all your rentals are cash-flowing.

DSCR eliminates that problem. The qualification lives in the property, not in you. An investor with 10 properties can qualify for the 11th using only that property's income.

**No W-2 required. No tax returns required. No employment verification required.**

This is the loan that serious portfolio investors use to scale.

## 2026 DSCR Requirements

**DSCR Ratio:**
- Minimum: 1.00 (break-even — property income exactly covers debt)
- Standard minimum for most lenders: 1.20-1.25
- Best terms and rates: 1.25+
- Some lenders have raised minimums to 1.25 in 2026 (post-tightening)

**Credit Score:**
- Minimum: 640-660 FICO
- Best terms: 700+ FICO
- Higher LTV requires 700+

**Down Payment / LTV:**
- Standard: 20%-25% down (75%-80% LTV)
- Some lenders: up to 85% LTV with strong DSCR
- Short-term rental (Airbnb/VRBO): May require 25%-30% down

**Interest Rates (May 2026):**
- Range: 6.125%-9.125% depending on credit, LTV, DSCR, and property type
- Strong profile (740+ credit, 1.30+ DSCR, 75% LTV): Low-to-mid 6% range
- Weaker profile: 8%+ range

**Eligible Properties:**
- Single-family rentals (SFR) — the most common
- 2-4 unit properties
- Condos (warrantable and non-warrantable)
- Short-term rentals (with documentation requirements)
- Small multifamily (5-20 units at select lenders)

---

## How Rental Income Is Calculated

**Long-term rentals:** Actual lease agreement or market rent appraisal from the appraiser — whichever is lower.

**Short-term rentals:** More complex. Some lenders use actual documented income (12 months of platform statements). Others use market rent only. Some exclude STR income entirely. This is an area where having a broker who knows which lender accepts which documentation is crucial.

**Vacancy factor:** Most lenders apply a 5%-10% vacancy haircut to the gross rental income before calculating DSCR. So if your property rents for $2,500, they may calculate DSCR on $2,250-$2,375.

---

## DSCR vs. Conventional Investment Loan

| Feature | DSCR | Conventional Investment |
|---|---|---|
| Income documentation | None | Full (W-2s, tax returns, 2 years) |
| Qualification basis | Property cash flow | Personal income + all properties |
| Scalability | Excellent | Degrades with each property |
| Rate | Slightly higher | Slightly lower |
| Best for | Portfolio builders | First or second investment property |

---

## What to Watch Out For

**2026 underwriting tightening:** Lenders have been tightening NOI calculations — adding management fee deductions, vacancy factors, and repair reserves to the DSCR calculation even when the property is self-managed. A property that "penciled" at 1.25 DSCR in 2024 may calculate at 1.15 under 2026 standards.

**Short-term rental risk:** Lenders are increasingly skeptical of projected Airbnb income. Bring documented history, not projections.

**Prepayment penalties:** Many DSCR loans have prepayment penalties (typically 3-5 years). Understand this before you sign.
