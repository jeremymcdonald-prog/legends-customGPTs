## What Is a VA Loan

A VA loan is a mortgage benefit backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. It is not a direct loan from the government — it is a guarantee. The VA tells approved lenders like me: "If this veteran defaults, we'll cover a portion of your loss." That guarantee is what allows lenders to offer veterans better terms than conventional borrowers typically get.

The guarantee gives veterans access to:
- Zero down payment (no other loan type offers this without income limits)
- No private mortgage insurance (PMI) — ever
- Competitive interest rates (often 0.25%-0.50% below conventional)
- More flexible credit qualifying standards
- Limits on what closing costs veterans can be charged
- The ability to assume the loan (transfer to another buyer)

## Who Qualifies

**Active Duty:** 90 consecutive days of service during wartime, or 181 days during peacetime.

**Veterans:** Same service duration thresholds — 90 days wartime, 181 days peacetime, with an honorable discharge (or general discharge under honorable conditions).

**National Guard and Reserve:** 6 years of service, OR 90 days of non-training active duty under Title 10 or Title 32 (this includes COVID-19 response, natural disaster response, etc.).

**Surviving Spouses:** Un-remarried surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability. Surviving spouses who remarried after age 57 may also qualify.

**What you need to apply:** Certificate of Eligibility (COE). I pull this for my clients — it takes 5 minutes if you're in the DEERS system. You don't need to figure this out yourself.

## The Zero-Down Advantage (And What It Actually Means)

Zero down payment means exactly that — you can purchase a home with no money down if you have full VA entitlement. Not 3.5% down like FHA. Not 3% down like conventional. Zero.

On a $350,000 home in Jacksonville, that's $12,250 (3.5% FHA) to $70,000 (20% conventional) that stays in your pocket.

**The PMI factor:** On a conventional loan with less than 20% down, you'll pay PMI — typically 0.5%-1.5% of the loan amount per year. On a $350,000 loan, that's $145-$437 per month in fees that build no equity and benefit nobody but the lender. VA loans never have PMI. Never.

## The VA Funding Fee — What It Is and Who's Exempt

This is the one "catch" with VA loans, so let's be honest about it. The VA charges a funding fee to help sustain the program for future generations. For 2026:

- First use, 0% down: **2.15%** of loan amount
- First use, 5%+ down: **1.50%**
- First use, 10%+ down: **1.25%**
- Subsequent use, 0% down: **3.30%**
- Subsequent use, 5%+ down: **1.50%**

The fee is financed into the loan — you don't pay it at closing. On a $350,000 loan with first-use, zero-down: that's $7,525 added to the loan balance. Spread over 30 years, that's about $40/month — still far less than PMI would cost.

**Who's exempt (no funding fee):**
- Veterans with a 10% or higher service-connected disability rating
- Veterans entitled to compensation but receiving retirement or active duty pay instead
- Purple Heart recipients
- Surviving spouses receiving DIC (Dependency and Indemnity Compensation)

If you're exempt, that's $7,500+ in savings right there.

## VA Loan Entitlement — The Part Nobody Explains Well

Your VA benefit comes with "entitlement" — essentially the amount the VA will guarantee. There are two types:

**Full entitlement:** If you've never used a VA loan before (or you've paid off a previous VA loan and had the entitlement restored), you have full entitlement. This means there are NO loan limits. You can borrow what you qualify for, not what a number says you can.

**Partial entitlement:** If you have an active VA loan or previously used your benefit without full restoration, your remaining entitlement limits your zero-down purchasing power. The limit in most areas is tied to the 2026 conforming loan limit of $832,750.

**Can you use your VA benefit more than once?** Yes. This is one of the biggest myths. You can restore full entitlement when you sell the home and pay off the VA loan. Or you can use remaining entitlement for a second purchase simultaneously (you can have two VA loans at once under the right conditions).

## Common VA Loan Myths — Debunked

**Myth: Sellers won't accept VA loans because they're harder to close.**
Truth: This was a valid concern 15 years ago. Today, VA loans close at rates comparable to conventional loans with most experienced lenders. The key is working with a lender (broker) who knows VA inside and out. I do.

**Myth: VA loans take forever to close.**
Truth: With a prepared borrower and an experienced lender, VA loans close in 21-30 days — same as most conventional loans.

**Myth: You can only use a VA loan once.**
Truth: False. You can use it multiple times, subject to entitlement availability.

**Myth: The VA appraisal is stricter and will kill deals.**
Truth: VA appraisals have minimum property requirements (MPR), but these protect the veteran. A home that fails VA appraisal is often one the veteran shouldn't buy anyway. Most standard homes pass without issue.

**Myth: You need a perfect credit score.**
Truth: The VA has no official minimum credit score. Lenders set their own minimums — typically 580-620. I have access to lenders on the more flexible end of that range.
