# BRRRR Strategy — Jeremy's Financing Guide

BRRRR stands for Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat. It is the most powerful systematic wealth-building strategy in residential real estate, and the financing layer is where most investors either win or lose. Let me walk you through it.

---

## Why BRRRR Works in 2026

Fix-and-flip ROI dropped to 23.1% in 2025 — lowest since 2008. The exit risk (selling to a buyer) is real in a high-rate market. BRRRR eliminates the sale risk: you exit the rehab with a tenant and a cash-flowing property, not a buyer-dependent sale.

The core genius: if you buy right and rehab right, you can pull all or most of your original capital out through the refinance, redeploy that capital into the next property, and keep the cash-flowing rental.

Done right, you're building a portfolio with the same dollars, over and over.

## The BRRRR Steps — Financing Focus

### Step 1: BUY (Distressed/Undervalued Property)

**The 70% Rule:**
Maximum purchase price = (After Repair Value × 0.70) − Estimated Repair Costs

Example:
- ARV: $250,000
- Estimated repairs: $35,000
- Maximum offer: ($250,000 × 0.70) − $35,000 = $140,000

**Financing options at this stage:**
- **Hard money loans:** Asset-based, fast approval, 10%-13% rates, 12-month terms. Best for most BRRRR buyers who need speed and flexibility.
- **Fix-and-flip loans:** Similar to hard money but sometimes from portfolio lenders with slightly better terms.
- **Cash:** Cleanest option if you have it; eliminates financing cost during rehab phase.
- **Private lender:** A personal relationship lender (often another investor) who provides bridge capital.
- **HELOC on existing property:** Draw from equity in your primary home or other investment property.

Key: Close fast. Distressed properties attract cash buyers. A 7-10 day close beats a 30-day financed close in a competitive acquisition.

### Step 2: REHAB (Add Value, Control Budget)

Financing during rehab is typically included in the hard money loan (drawn in stages as work is completed). Scope creep is the #1 killer of BRRRR deals — get three contractor bids, add a 15%-20% contingency buffer, and stick to the plan.

Focus rehab spending on what moves the appraisal needle:
- Kitchen and bathroom updates (highest ROI)
- Flooring (major visual impact, manageable cost)
- Curb appeal (first impression for tenants and appraisers)
- Functional systems (roof, HVAC, electrical, plumbing — necessary but doesn't boost value much beyond standard condition)

### Step 3: RENT (Stabilize the Asset)

Most lenders require a signed lease and rental income history before they'll do the cash-out refinance. A tenant in place transforms the property from a speculative project to an income-producing asset — and the appraisal reflects that.

Screen tenants thoroughly. The wrong tenant can derail your refinance timeline (if they stop paying) and damage the asset.

### Step 4: REFINANCE (Extract Capital)

**The exit refinance is the heart of the strategy.** This is where you convert your short-term acquisition financing into long-term permanent debt and pull out the capital you invested.

**Typical refinance structure:**
- Loan type: DSCR loan or conventional investment loan
- LTV: 75%-80% of the new appraised (post-rehab) value
- Seasoning requirement: Most lenders require 6-12 months of ownership before they'll use the new appraised value (not the purchase price)
- DSCR check: The property must cash flow at 1.20x+ to qualify at most lenders

**The math that makes it work:**
- Bought at $140,000, put in $35,000 in rehab = $175,000 invested
- Property appraised at $250,000 (ARV confirmed)
- Refinance at 75% LTV = $187,500 loan
- Cash out: $187,500 − (payoff of hard money loan) = capital returned
- If hard money loan balance is $175,000, you pull out $12,500 AND you have a cash-flowing rental

If you bought at 60% of ARV or better, you may recover all of your invested capital.

**DSCR refinance requirements (2026):**
- Minimum DSCR: 1.20-1.25
- Credit: 640+ (700+ for best terms)
- Minimum 20%-25% equity post-refinance
- 6-month seasoning at most lenders

### Step 5: REPEAT

Take the extracted capital and fund the next acquisition. The goal is to compound — each refinanced property funds the next, which funds the next.

## Common BRRRR Mistakes

**Overestimating ARV:** The appraisal is objective; your optimism is not. Use comparable sales within the last 90 days within 0.5 miles. If comps don't support the ARV, the math doesn't work.

**Underestimating rehab costs:** Always get three contractor bids. Always add 15%-20% contingency. The renovation that costs $25,000 in the estimate costs $32,000 in reality 80% of the time.

**Ignoring the seasoning period:** Most lenders won't refinance based on post-rehab value until you've owned the property 6-12 months. Plan your cash flow to cover the hard money loan cost during that period.

**DSCR miscalculation:** Calculate DSCR at the refinance rate (6%-8%), not at a hopeful future rate. The property needs to cash flow at today's financing costs.

## BRRRR in Northeast Florida

Jacksonville has several submarkets with BRRRR-viable distressed inventory:
- Northside Jacksonville (Brentwood, Moncrief, Paxon) — older housing stock, active distressed market
- Westside (Murray Hill, Mixon Town) — gentrifying areas with upside potential
- Parts of Duval County outside the premium coastal markets

Key: competition from iBuyers and institutional investors has reduced the margin in many Florida markets. The best deals come from the MLS within the first 24 hours, off-market relationships, and probate/estate situations.
