# FHA Loans — Jeremy's Complete Guide

FHA loans get a bad reputation they don't deserve. They're not "the loan you get when you can't get anything else." They're a legitimate, well-designed product that makes homeownership possible for millions of people who otherwise couldn't get there. Here's the honest breakdown.

---

## What Is an FHA Loan

FHA stands for Federal Housing Administration. Like VA, FHA doesn't lend money directly — the FHA insures the loan, which reduces the lender's risk and allows more flexible qualifying standards.

FHA loans are designed for:
- First-time buyers with limited down payment savings
- Buyers with lower credit scores who can't qualify for conventional
- Buyers with higher debt-to-income ratios
- Buyers who want the flexibility of gift funds for the down payment

---

## 2026 FHA Requirements

**Credit Scores:**
- 580+ FICO: 3.5% minimum down payment
- 500-579 FICO: 10% minimum down payment
- Below 500: Not eligible

Most lenders set their practical minimum at 580-620. I have access to lenders who will go to 580.

**Down Payment:**
- 3.5% minimum (with 580+ score)
- 10% minimum (with 500-579 score)
- 100% of down payment can come from gift funds — parents, relatives, employer, charity

**Debt-to-Income (DTI):**
- Maximum 43% (standard)
- Up to 57% with compensating factors (strong reserves, high credit score, etc.)

**2026 FHA Loan Limits:**
- Standard (most areas): $541,287 for single-family homes
- High-cost markets: Up to $1,249,125
- Jacksonville area limits are at standard levels

**Primary Residence Requirement:** FHA loans are for owner-occupied primary residences only. You cannot use FHA to buy an investment property.

## FHA Mortgage Insurance Premiums (MIP) — The Full Story

This is the main trade-off of FHA vs. conventional, and you need to understand it fully.

**Upfront MIP:** 1.75% of the loan amount, added to your loan balance. On a $300,000 loan, that's $5,250 financed into the loan.

**Annual MIP (paid monthly):**
- Down payment less than 10%: 0.55% per year for the LIFE of the loan
- Down payment 10%+: 0.55% per year for 11 years, then cancels

On a $300,000 loan with 3.5% down: annual MIP = $1,650/year = $137.50/month. Forever.

**How this compares to PMI (conventional):**
Conventional PMI cancels when you reach 20% equity — typically in 7-10 years with normal payments. FHA MIP with less than 10% down never cancels unless you refinance.

**The FHA-to-Conventional Refinance Strategy:**
Many buyers start with FHA (lower credit score required, lower down payment) and refinance to conventional once their credit improves and they've built equity. This eliminates the ongoing MIP. I plan this transition with my FHA clients from day one.

## When FHA Makes Sense

- Your credit score is 580-619 (below conventional-accessible range)
- Your down payment is under 5% and you want the lowest monthly payment
- Your DTI is above 43% (FHA allows more flexibility with compensating factors)
- You're receiving all gift funds for the down payment
- You've had a bankruptcy or foreclosure that's within the conventional seasoning period but outside FHA's shorter seasoning period

---

## When FHA Doesn't Make Sense

- Your credit score is 640+ and you have 5%+ down — conventional may give you lower total costs
- You're buying a property that won't pass FHA minimum property requirements (heavy fixer-uppers)
- You're buying above the FHA loan limit for your area
- You plan to stay in the home long-term and want to avoid lifetime MIP
