# Jeremy McDonald — Story Bank

Stories close. Facts inform. The best content uses both, but stories are what people remember and share. These are my 10 core story structures. Each one is a template that I can adapt to any topic.

---

## Story Structure 1: The Football Coach Moment

**Setup:** I coached football for years after playing. Football taught me something that translates directly to mortgage: preparation beats talent when it's the 4th quarter and the game is on the line.

**How to adapt:**
- *For first-time buyers:* "Getting a mortgage is like a 4th quarter drive. The preparation you do in the first three quarters — credit, down payment, documents — is what determines whether you score when it matters. Most buyers scramble in Q4 because nobody coached them in Q1."
- *For credit repair:* "In football, you don't fix your O-line the night before the game. You fix it in training camp. Six months from now you can have a completely different credit profile — but only if you start the work now."
- *For rate shopping:* "A good coach scouts every opponent. He doesn't assume the team in front of him is the only one worth studying. I scout 40+ lenders for every client. That's coaching."

**Core message:** Preparation, strategy, and the right guidance at the right time determine outcomes. Not luck.

## Story Structure 2: The Bodybuilder's Discipline

**Setup:** I'm training for bodybuilding competition. The discipline required is relentless — consistent nutrition, consistent training, no shortcuts. Results don't lie and the timeline can't be compressed past a certain point.

**How to adapt:**
- *For credit improvement:* "I hear this in the gym all the time: 'Is there a faster way?' There's always a faster way that leads to an injury. Credit works the same way. There are legitimate techniques that move your score 30-50 points in 60 days. There are no techniques that move it 200 points in a week. Anyone promising that is trying to take your money."
- *For down payment savings:* "Saving a down payment is a bodybuilder's game. The people who hit their savings goal in 12 months are the people who treated it like a training program — consistent amounts, monthly, non-negotiable. The people who 'save when they can' are still renting in three years."
- *For the long-term mortgage view:* "Every mortgage payment is a rep. 360 reps on a 30-year loan. Each one builds equity. The people who stick to the program — who don't cash-out refi every 3 years just to buy toys — end up with a paid-off house. Discipline compounds."

**Core message:** Discipline, consistency, and patience beat shortcuts every time. The timeline is real and it's worth respecting.

## Story Structure 3: The World Traveler's Perspective

**Setup:** I've traveled extensively — seen how people in different countries approach homeownership, wealth building, and financial systems. That global perspective gives me context that most loan officers don't have.

**How to adapt:**
- *For broker advantage:* "In most developed countries, mortgage brokering is the dominant model — not retail bank lending. Consumers in the UK and Australia use brokers at 70%+ rates. Americans are still learning what most of the world figured out decades ago: you need someone in your corner, not someone on the bank's payroll."
- *For VA loans:* "I've been to countries where the government provides meaningful housing benefits to its military families. Some of those benefits are modest. The VA loan — zero down, no PMI, competitive rates — is extraordinary by any global standard. Veterans who don't use it are leaving one of the best government programs in the world on the table."
- *For the long-term view:* "In many parts of the world, homeownership is generational. People buy once, raise their family, and pass the home to their children. Americans trade homes like cards. I'm not saying that's wrong — but the families I've seen build the most wealth are the ones who bought, stayed, and let equity compound over decades."

**Core message:** Global perspective gives us context for what's actually valuable about American homeownership — and the VA benefit in particular.

## Story Structure 4: The 20-Year Finance Journey

**Setup:** I've been in mortgage for 20 years. I've seen two full market cycles. I know what a good market, a bad market, a rate spike, and a rate crash look like from the inside.

**How to adapt:**
- *For rate perspective:* "I've been doing this through 3% rates and through 7% rates. You know what I noticed? People who bought at 7% in the early 2000s and refinanced to 4% in 2012 had the same house they would have bought at 3% — just with a couple of extra refinances on the way. They didn't miss the market. They were in the market. That's the advantage."
- *For market timing:* "In 20 years, I have not once successfully predicted the exact bottom of a rate cycle. Neither has any economist I've met. What I have learned: the people who try to time the bottom often miss the house. The people who buy when they're ready always have a house."
- *For client trust:* "I'm NMLS licensed. That license requires ongoing education and is tied to my career and reputation. When I give you advice, I'm giving you the same advice I'd give my own family — because if you're unhappy, you don't refer me anyone, and I'm out of business."

**Core message:** Two decades of experience is wisdom that credentials can't replicate. Pattern recognition earned through doing, not studying.

## Story Structure 5: Client Win Stories (Anonymized)

**The Marcus Story (VA Loan):**
Marcus was an Army veteran, 12 years served, preparing to put $30,000 down on a $320,000 home. His bank had him convinced he needed the down payment to qualify. I showed him he had full VA entitlement — zero down, no PMI. He kept his $30,000, bought the house at a lower monthly payment than the bank quoted (VA rate vs. conventional), and used the savings to furnish the home debt-free. He sends me a referral every year.

**The Sarah Story (First-Time Buyer, Credit Obstacle):**
Sarah was a 28-year-old teacher with a 591 credit score — above the FHA minimum but not where I wanted it. Instead of just processing her application, I spent 45 minutes looking at her credit report with her. We found two errors (medical collections she didn't recognize) and one high-utilization card. We disputed the errors through rapid rescore, she paid down the card, and 30 days later her score was 624. Same loan, better rate, lower monthly payment. She closed on her first home. She refers me to every teacher in her school.

**The Rodriguez Family (BRRRR/Investor):**
The Rodriguez family owned their primary home with significant equity. They wanted to build an investment portfolio but thought they needed cash. I introduced them to the DSCR loan structure — qualifying based on rental income, not their W-2s. They BRRRR'd two properties in 18 months using cash-out refinances and DSCR financing. They now have three doors generating monthly cash flow. All from equity they already owned.

**The Johnson's (Refinance Story):**
The Johnsons bought in 2023 at 7.2%. They called me when rates hit 6.1% in early 2026. The math worked: $160/month savings, breakeven on closing costs in 14 months. We refinanced them in 28 days. They plan to refinance again when rates hit 5.5%. They're playing the "date the rate" strategy exactly right.

## Story Structure 6: The Father of Three Frame

**Setup:** I have three kids. My decisions about homeownership, finance, and wealth aren't abstract — they're personal. I'm playing the same game my clients are playing.

**How to adapt:**
- *For the long-term view:* "When I think about mortgage advice, I think about what I'd tell my own kids. Would I tell them to overpay at their bank because it's convenient? No. Would I tell them to wait for rates to be perfect before buying? No. I'd tell them to get the best deal available today and build equity while they wait for a better one."
- *For urgency without pressure:* "I have three kids. I know what it costs to wait. Every year in rent is a year of equity building for someone else. I'm not saying rush. I'm saying understand the cost of waiting and make that decision with full information."

**Core message:** My advice is personal, not transactional. I'm building the same financial future for my family that I'm helping you build for yours.

## Story Structure 7: Tony Robbins / Personal Development Frame

**Setup:** I've been to Tony Robbins' Unleash the Power Within (UPW) and have studied Atomic Habits, Can't Hurt Me, and other peak performance content. That mental framework shapes how I approach both my business and my clients' financial journeys.

**How to adapt:**
- *For credit repair mindset:* "David Goggins talks about the 40% rule — most people quit at 40% of their actual capacity. Credit improvement is the same. People give up after 60 days because they don't see enough progress. The compounding happens in months 3-6. The people who hit their credit goals are the ones who stayed in the race."
- *For overcoming fear:* "Tony Robbins says the quality of your life is the quality of your emotions. The people who build the most wealth through real estate are the ones who made the decision — the committed decision — to stop letting fear run their financial life. That's not motivational speak. That's what I watch happen with my clients every year."
- *For identity change:* "Atomic Habits is about identity before habits. You don't save money as a behavior — you become a saver as an identity. The same applies to homeownership. The people who get there fastest are the ones who start seeing themselves as homeowners before they technically are. That identity shift changes every financial decision that follows."

**Core message:** Peak performance principles apply to financial decision-making. Mindset and identity precede outcomes.

## Story Structure 8: The Skeptic's Conversion

**Setup:** I have seen countless clients who came to me skeptical — of brokers, of the process, of their own ability to qualify — and walked away not just helped but converted. Their story is the most powerful marketing I have.

**How to adapt:**
- *For broker skeptics:* "Mike came to me after three banks told him he didn't qualify. He was done trying. He called me as a last resort, half-convinced I was just going to be bank number four. We closed in 29 days. The issue wasn't his income or credit — it was that banks were using their overlay guidelines, not the actual agency guidelines. I found a lender without the overlay. Mike has referred me 11 people in three years."
- *For rate skeptics:* "Jennifer already had a pre-approval from her credit union at 6.75%. She was ready to sign. Her realtor convinced her to call me first. Twenty-four hours later she had a lock at 6.28%. Same loan. Same timeline. $152 less per month. Jennifer's credit union was not trying to rip her off — they just couldn't compete with what wholesale lenders offered me."

**Core message:** Skepticism is healthy. Real results convert skeptics into advocates. The proof is in the outcomes.

## Story Structure 9: The "What They Don't Tell You" Frame

**Setup:** Twenty years in finance means I've seen the industry from the inside. I know what banks optimize for, what gets hidden in the fine print, and what the average consumer never thinks to ask.

**How to adapt:**
- *For closing costs:* "Nobody sits down with first-time buyers and explains that closing costs exist, let alone what they are. So the first time most people hear about $10,000-$17,000 in additional costs is 3 days before closing. That is not a coincidence — people who don't know to ask don't ask. I'm going to tell you before you start looking."
- *For rate shopping:* "Lenders know that 73% of borrowers accept the first rate they're quoted. They price accordingly. They're not evil — they're just running a business. My business model is built on the other 27%, the people who know to shop. Let me help you be in that group."

**Core message:** Information asymmetry is the lender's biggest advantage. Closing that gap is what I do.

## Story Structure 10: The AI-Empowers-Humans Frame

**Setup:** I use AI to give every client a better experience. That's not replacing judgment — it's amplifying it. I can analyze more data, catch more patterns, and respond faster because I'm using the right tools.

**How to adapt:**
- *For tech-savvy clients:* "I use AI to compare rates across 40+ lenders in real time, to analyze your credit profile against multiple loan programs simultaneously, and to draft your pre-approval letter within hours of receiving your documents. The technology makes me faster. The 20 years of experience makes me right."
- *For skeptical clients:* "Some people hear 'AI' and think 'robot is making decisions about my mortgage.' That's not how I use it. I use AI the way a surgeon uses imaging technology — it gives me better data to make better decisions. I'm still the one making the call."
- *For content/education:* "Part of why I make these videos is because AI has changed the information landscape dramatically. There is more content about mortgages than ever, and most of it is wrong, generic, or designed to sell you something. My goal is to be the one source you trust for Northeast Florida mortgage information — real numbers, real market data, real advice."

**Core message:** AI amplifies human expertise. It doesn't replace it. The judgment is still mine — the tools just make it faster and more accurate.
