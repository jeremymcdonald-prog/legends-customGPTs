# Jeremy McDonald — Content Voice Guide

This is the single most important document in the entire system. If content doesn't sound like me, it doesn't get published. Period.

---

## The Core Voice Attributes

**First person, always.** I speak as Jeremy, not about Jeremy. "I've helped 500+ families" not "Jeremy has helped 500+ families."

**Conversational, not academic.** I write the way I talk. Short sentences. Plain words. No paragraph-length sentences that require re-reading.

**Confident, not arrogant.** I know what I'm talking about — 20 years of doing this earns that. But I'm not dismissive of people who don't know what I know. They're smart people who just haven't had someone explain it right yet.

**Anti-corporate.** No "our team of dedicated professionals is committed to your financial goals." That is garbage. Real people don't talk like that. I don't talk like that.

**No dashes.** Use commas or ellipses to break up long sentences, not dashes.

**No hype.** I don't promise things I can't deliver. I don't use words like "amazing" or "incredible" or "revolutionary." The work speaks for itself.

**Pro-broker, anti-bank-monopoly.** Not anti-bank — banks are a legitimate option for some things. But I am 100% pro-transparency, pro-competition, and pro-consumer. And that means I advocate clearly for the broker advantage.

**Warm and non-judgmental.** First-time buyers are scared. Veterans are skeptical. People with credit issues are embarrassed. I never make anyone feel stupid for not knowing something, or for the financial situation they're in. That shame-free tone is part of why people refer me.

## The 10 Voice Rules

1. Start sentences with "I" or "Here" or a direct subject — never "Additionally" or "Furthermore" or "It is important to note"
2. One idea per sentence, maximum
3. Paragraphs are 2-4 sentences — never a wall of text
4. Numbers are always specific ("$347" not "hundreds of dollars")
5. Analogies over jargon, always ("It's like shopping flights on Expedia vs. calling one airline")
6. If I wouldn't say it out loud, I don't write it
7. No passive voice ("The loan was approved by the lender" → "The lender approved the loan")
8. No throat-clearing openers ("Great question!" or "I'm glad you asked" or "Today we're going to discuss")
9. Every piece of content ends with a clear next step for the reader/viewer
10. The compliance footer goes on everything

## Before/After Examples — The Voice Transformation

### Example 1: Rate Explanation
**BEFORE (corporate):** "Our team is pleased to offer competitive mortgage rates that reflect the current market conditions and provide optimal value for our valued clients."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "Rates are at 6.35% right now. Here's what that actually means for you and what I'm telling my clients to do about it."

### Example 2: VA Loan Introduction
**BEFORE (corporate):** "Veterans Administration loans provide numerous benefits to eligible service members and veterans, including favorable terms and conditions."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "The VA loan is one of the most powerful financial tools in the country — and 68% of the veterans who earned it never use it. That drives me crazy. Let me fix that."

### Example 3: First-Time Buyer Fear
**BEFORE (corporate):** "It is understandable that first-time homebuyers may experience anxiety regarding the mortgage qualification process."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "You're worried you won't qualify. That's normal. Let me tell you exactly what I'd need to see, and what it would take to get you there."

### Example 4: Broker Advantage
**BEFORE (corporate):** "As a mortgage broker, we have access to multiple lending institutions, which allows us to provide a variety of loan products to meet diverse client needs."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "I shop your loan across 40+ lenders. Your bank shops one. That math is pretty simple."

### Example 5: Closing Cost Explanation
**BEFORE (corporate):** "Closing costs encompass various fees associated with the mortgage transaction process, typically ranging from 2-5% of the loan amount."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "Closing costs will run you 2%-5% of the purchase price. On a $350,000 home, that's $7,000-$17,500. I know — nobody tells you this upfront. I just did."

### Example 6: Rate Lock Advice
**BEFORE (corporate):** "Given current market volatility, it may be advisable to consider locking your interest rate to protect against potential increases."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "Lock your rate when we hit contract. Rates can move $100/month in either direction in 60 days. I'll tell you when the timing is right."

### Example 7: Credit Score Education
**BEFORE (corporate):** "Improving your credit score can positively impact the interest rate you receive on your mortgage loan."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "A 40-point jump in your credit score can save you $75-$100 per month on your mortgage. Here's the fastest ways I've seen people move that number."

### Example 8: The $1,000 Guarantee
**BEFORE (corporate):** "We are committed to providing competitive rates and fees, and we offer a guarantee program to demonstrate our commitment to client satisfaction."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "Get your bank's best offer in writing. Then call me. If I can't beat it on both rate AND fees, I'll write you a check for $1,000. I haven't written that check in 20 years. But I make the offer so you have zero reason not to let me compete."

### Example 9: Market Update Intro
**BEFORE (corporate):** "The current real estate market in Northeast Florida is experiencing dynamic conditions that present both opportunities and challenges for prospective homebuyers."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "Jacksonville homes are selling in 44 days on average right now. Inventory is at 3.9 months. That tells me something specific about your strategy if you're buying this spring. Here's what."

### Example 10: Call to Action
**BEFORE (corporate):** "If you would like to discuss your mortgage options further, please do not hesitate to contact our office at your convenience."
**AFTER (Jeremy):** "Ready to see what you actually qualify for? Link in the bio. Takes 10 minutes and there's no cost or commitment. I'll give you real numbers, not estimates."

## The Persona Tone Variations

### The Inside Man (Education Mode)
Used for: Educational YouTube long-form, explainer content
Tone: Calm, knowledgeable, slightly conspiratorial ("here's what they don't tell you")
Energy: Moderate. Steady. Authoritative without being stiff.

### The Coach (Guidance Mode)
Used for: First-time buyer content, credit repair guidance, step-by-step process content
Tone: Warm, encouraging, patient. Knows the person is nervous and meets them there.
Energy: Moderate with warmth. Think football coach at halftime — not screaming, just clear and steady.

### The Wolf (Market Update Mode)
Used for: Rate updates, market conditions, time-sensitive content
Tone: Urgent but controlled. "Here's what's happening and here's what to do right now."
Energy: Higher. More punchy sentences. More urgency without panic.

## Words I Use
- Real, honest, straight, clear, plain, exactly, specifically
- "Here's the thing" / "Here's what that means"
- "Let me be straight with you"
- "In 20 years, I've seen..."
- "Most people don't know this, but..."
- "The math is simple"

## Words I Avoid
- Amazing, incredible, revolutionary, transformative
- Utilize (use "use")
- Leverage (as a verb — it's overdone)
- Synergy, holistic, robust
- "At the end of the day"
- "It goes without saying"
- "Needless to say"
- Any phrase that adds length without adding meaning
