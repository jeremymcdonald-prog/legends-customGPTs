# Thumbnail Brief: "$1,000 Guarantee" Video
## 3 Concepts for A/B Testing

**Video Title Options:**
- "I'll Pay You $1,000 If I'm Wrong About Your Mortgage Rate"
- "The $1,000 Mortgage Rate Guarantee (Most Lenders Would Never Do This)"
- "Why I Offer a $1,000 Guarantee on Every Loan — And Why Banks Won't"

---

## CONCEPT A: "Money On The Table"
*Primary recommendation — highest click potential*

**Visual Layout:**
- Background: Dark navy/charcoal (Inside Man palette)
- Left 40%: Jeremy's face — expression: confident half-smile, eyebrow slightly raised (the "I know something you don't" look)
- Right 60%: Large $1,000 bill graphic or bold "$1,000" in gold/yellow text
- Top banner: Red strip with white text: "BANKS WON'T DO THIS"
- Bottom: Secondary text in white: "Mortgage Rate Guarantee"

**Text Hierarchy:**
1. "$1,000" — massive, gold, center-right
2. "BANKS WON'T DO THIS" — red banner, white text
3. Jeremy's face — authority, left side

**Emotion Target:** Curiosity + mild outrage ("Why won't banks do this?")

**Color Palette:** Navy background, gold $1,000, red banner, white text

**Font Recommendations:** Impact or Anton (bold, uppercase) for "$1,000", Roboto Bold for banner text

**Face Direction:** Jeremy looking slightly toward the "$1,000" graphic (eyes directing viewer attention)

**Why This Works:** The knowledge gap is "why would someone PAY ME if I use them?" Combined with the bank angle, it triggers the curiosity-suspicion loop that gets clicks from homebuyers.

---

## CONCEPT B: "Side-By-Side Shock"
*Best for rate-focused audience*

**Visual Layout:**
- Split screen: Left side = "BANK" label with red X, higher rate number in red
- Right side = "BROKER" label with green checkmark, lower rate number in green
- Jeremy's face bottom-center, breaking the split, looking up at camera
- Top text: "YOUR BANK IS OVERCHARGING YOU"
- Bottom text in bold: "$16,000 Difference"

**Text Hierarchy:**
1. "YOUR BANK IS OVERCHARGING YOU" — top, white on dark, high contrast
2. Rate comparison numbers (e.g., "7.25% vs 6.50%") — large, color-coded
3. "$16,000 Difference" — bottom, gold text
4. Jeremy face — anchor/authority

**Emotion Target:** Alarm + validation ("I knew something was off")

**Color Palette:** Dark background, red for bank side, green for broker side, gold for the dollar amount

**Why This Works:** Direct comparison is the clearest value proposition. The dollar amount makes the abstract rate difference tangible. "Your bank is overcharging you" is an accusation, which creates emotional engagement.

**Risk:** Slightly more aggressive. Good for audience that's already skeptical of banks. May underperform with audience that has bank loyalty.

---

## CONCEPT C: "The Promise"
*Best for trust-building / personal brand*

**Visual Layout:**
- Clean, single-focus
- Jeremy front and center, confident smile, professional but approachable
- Large text overlay: "I'll Give You $1,000 If I'm Wrong"
- Subtext: "20 Years. Every Client. No Exceptions."
- Small NMLS disclaimer in corner (tiny, compliance)
- Background: Soft blur of Jacksonville skyline or professional office

**Text Hierarchy:**
1. "I'll Give You $1,000 If I'm Wrong" — dominant, white on semi-transparent overlay
2. "20 Years. Every Client." — smaller, gold
3. Jeremy's confident expression — the entire trust signal

**Emotion Target:** Trust + relief ("Finally, someone who's confident enough to back it up")

**Color Palette:** Warmer tones, white and gold text, less dark/aggressive than Concept A

**Why This Works:** Personal brand building. This thumbnail works best after the channel has some traction and viewers know who Jeremy is. It's the "relationship" thumbnail. Less about shock, more about authority and warmth.

**Best For:** Retargeting, email newsletter audiences, LinkedIn repurpose

---

## Testing Protocol

**Phase 1 (Days 1-3):** Launch with Concept A (Money On The Table)  
**Phase 2 (Day 4-7):** If CTR below 4%, swap to Concept B  
**Benchmark CTR:** 5%+ is strong for mortgage content, 3-5% is acceptable, below 3% test new thumbnail  
**Tool:** YouTube Studio > Reach > Impressions Click-Through Rate  

**File Specs for Production:**
- Resolution: 1280 x 720 pixels minimum (1920 x 1080 recommended)
- Format: JPG or PNG
- File size: Under 2MB
- Color space: RGB
